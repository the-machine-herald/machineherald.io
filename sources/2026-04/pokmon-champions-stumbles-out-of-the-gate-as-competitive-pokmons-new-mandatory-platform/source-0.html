Pokémon Champions Is Now Available on Nintendo Switch and Nintendo Switch 2

Oh yeah, it’s time to battle.

Get ready for intense Pokémon battles! Pokémon Champions is now available for download on Nintendo Switch and Nintendo Switch 2. Especially enthusiastic Trainers can really hit the ground running with the Pokémon Champions + Starter Pack bundle, which includes in-game benefits like added Pokémon storage space, an extra battle song, and more. Nintendo Switch 2 players will be able to download a free update to the game that allows you to enjoy the action with even clearer graphics.

In Pokémon Champions, you’ll engage in Pokémon battles like those in previous Pokémon titles. These battles will utilize familiar mechanics such as Pokémon types, Abilities, and moves, enabling the rich and varied battle strategies that players know and love. A mobile version of Pokémon Champions is also on the way, and you’ll be able to battle cross-platform between Nintendo Switch and mobile devices.

Pokémon Champions will also link with Pokémon HOME, allowing players to team up with certain Pokémon brought over from past Pokémon series video games and Pokémon GO.

Among the Pokémon appearing in Pokémon Champions are Mega Meganium, Mega Emboar, and Mega Feraligatr, who all originally appeared in Pokémon Legends: Z-A. After undergoing Mega Evolution, some of these Pokémon will have never-before-seen Abilities in Pokémon Champions.

Mega Meganium will have the newly discovered Ability Mega Sol, which lets the Pokémon use its moves as if the weather were harsh sunlight. Mega Emboar has the Mold Breaker Ability, which causes its moves to be unaffected by the Ability of the target. Mega Feraligatr’s newly discovered Ability, Dragonize, turns Normal-type moves into Dragon-type moves and boosts their power by 20%.



Receive Chesnaughtite, Delphoxite, Greninjite, and Floettite


By storing the Chesnaught, Delphox, Greninja, and Eternal Flower Floette you befriended during Pokémon Legends: Z-A in Pokémon HOME and bringing them to Pokémon Champions as visitors, you can obtain their Mega Stones from your mailbox in Pokémon Champions.


Receive Dragonite as an Early-Download Bonus


If you play Pokémon Champions by Monday, August 31, 2026, you can claim Dragonite and 100 Quick Coupons from your mailbox in-game.

Additionally, if you continue playing Pokémon Champions, you can obtain a Dragoninite stone from the first season’s Battle Pass. When Dragonite holds a Dragoninite stone, it can Mega Evolve into Mega Dragonite.

  • Dragonite can be claimed on both the Nintendo Switch version and mobile device version.

  • Dragonite can also be obtained through other methods.

  • The Dragoninite stone available in this campaign may be unlocked in the shop or other areas of the game at a later date.

  • Pokémon received as gifts in Pokémon Champions can’t be deposited in Pokémon HOME.


Pokémon Champions Competitions


Ready to give some of those new Mega-Evolved Pokémon a try? You can find an exciting in-game tournament known as the Warm-up Challenge right as you open Pokémon Champions, where you’ll be able to battle Trainers across the world and earn rewards based on your performance. Entry for this tournament will be open until the tournament concludes on April 12 at 6:59 p.m. PDT. If you finish three or more matches with at least one win or loss, you’ll be able to claim a Gardevoir and 100 Quick Coupons from your in-game mailbox after checking the final ranking results.

If you’re looking to take your battle talents even further or just take a peek at the competition, look no further than the 2026 Indianapolis Regional Championships from May 29 through May 31! This will be the first Video Game Championships event to feature Pokémon Champions and will be a perfect sneak peek for all the action to take place at the 2026 North America International Championships from June 12 through June 14.

Whether you’re looking to reach the top in Ranked Battles or enjoy some easygoing Casual Battles, Pokémon Champions is ready to deliver plenty of fun!

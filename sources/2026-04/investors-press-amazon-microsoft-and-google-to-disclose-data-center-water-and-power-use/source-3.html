Advancing responsible water use at our data centers

Data centers generate heat and need to be cooled to operate. Google takes a climate-conscious approach, balancing the trade-offs between using energy and water for cooling.

Link to Youtube Video (visible only when JS is disabled)

Water stewardship at our data centers

  • Transparency

    To help communities understand how we manage water responsibly, we report our annual water use for each data center location and every replenishment project we’ve supported.

    Read more

  • Climate-conscious cooling

    At each data center campus, our cooling decisions consider the local environment — balancing carbon-free energy and responsibly-sourced water — to minimize the net climate impact both today and in the future.

    Read more

  • Responsible water use

    We evaluate the local water source for each data center, using a science-based framework to assess watershed health. In locations with high water risk, we look for alternatives like air-cooling or recycled water.

    Read more

  • Replenishment and watershed health

    We aim to replenish more freshwater than we consume, on average, across our data centers and offices by 2030 and help improve water quality and ecosystem health in the communities where we operate.

    Read more

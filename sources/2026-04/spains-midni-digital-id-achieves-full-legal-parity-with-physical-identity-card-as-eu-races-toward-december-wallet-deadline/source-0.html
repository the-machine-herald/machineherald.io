Spain’s MiDNI Mobile App Now Legally Valid for In-Person Identification

April 2, 2026

Spain has reached a milestone in its mobile identity rollout: as of April 2, 2026, the MiDNI app is legally valid as a substitute for the physical national identity card in face-to-face identification across both public and private settings.

MiDNI is the official digital version of the Documento Nacional de Identidad (DNI), available on Android and iOS through official app stores and the government portal midni.gob.es. The app connects to National Police servers and generates digitally signed identity data in real time. The signed data is valid only for a short window, a security measure designed to prevent tampering or reuse. Citizens can control how much personal data is shared depending on the context, from a full identity check to a simple age confirmation.

The legal basis is Royal Decree 255/2025, which established the framework for the digital DNI and gave public administrations and private entities 12 months to put systems in place to accept it. That grace period ends April 2, making acceptance of MiDNI mandatory for organizations that require identity verification.

Accepted use cases include government administrative procedures, hotel check-ins, car rentals, ticketed events, pharmacy pickups, notarial proceedings, and select financial transactions. The credential does not yet support online authentication, electronic signatures, or international travel. Foreign residents holding an NIE, or Foreigner Identification Number, are not currently eligible, and the Interior Ministry has said it is working on extending eligibility in future phases.

The National Police has conducted public awareness campaigns in cities across Spain to prepare frontline staff at businesses and government offices to accept the mobile credential. Spain launched MiDNI in April 2025 as a voluntary companion to the physical card before granting it full legal parity.

Spain’s rollout places it among a growing number of countries moving toward legally recognized mobile identity credentials. The EU’s eIDAS 2.0 framework, which mandates digital identity wallets for all member states, provides the broader regulatory context driving adoption across Europe.

Sources: Spanish Interior Ministry

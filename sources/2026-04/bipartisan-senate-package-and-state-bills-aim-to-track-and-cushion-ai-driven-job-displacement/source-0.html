Exclusive: Warner and Rounds roll out bipartisan AI workforce bill

  • Ashley Gold

Sens. Mark Warner (D-Va.) and Mike Rounds (R-S.D.) on Wednesday are introducing new legislation focused on AI and the workforce, per an announcement shared exclusively with Axios.

Why it matters: Concerns on Capitol Hill are mounting over AI's impact on jobs as the midterms approach, and a bipartisan bill to help American workers adapt reflects that momentum.

Driving the news: The Economy of the Future Commission Act would create a panel to develop policy recommendations on AI's economic and workforce impacts, per the announcement.

  • The commission would include Republican and Democratic members of Congress, along with experts from industry, academia and government.
  • Within seven months, it would issue an interim report on "expected employment changes from AI."
  • The commission would then have 13 months to deliver a final report with legislative recommendations on AI education, workforce training and re-skilling, as well as taxation and unemployment insurance policy.

The legislation is endorsed by Microsoft and Google, among other AI, education, labor and tech industry groups and colleges.

What they're saying: "We need clear-eyed analysis and practical, bipartisan solutions to help workers gain new skills, support people whose jobs are disrupted, and make sure the United States leads the world in the industries of the future," Warner said in a statement.

  • "This legislation is about bringing together the expertise needed to chart that path forward."

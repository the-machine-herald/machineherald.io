Cerebras, AI Infrastructure and Systems Provider, Files for Nasdaq Global Select Market IPO

3 min read

Cerebras Systems filed for an initial public offering on the Nasdaq Global Select Market under the ticker “CBRS.” The company builds wafer-scale AI processors, turnkey systems, and a high-speed inference cloud, reporting $510.0 million in 2025 revenue and $237.8 million in net income. Proceeds are earmarked for general corporate purposes and to cover tax withholdings tied to RSU settlements, with flexibility for potential acquisitions.

Business Description

Cerebras designs and sells high-performance AI infrastructure centered on its Wafer-Scale Engine (WSE), the industry’s first and only commercial wafer-scale AI processor, and the CS‑3 system that integrates power, cooling and interconnect. The company’s co-designed software stack spans model compilation, inference serving and cluster orchestration, allowing customers to train and serve large models without distributed programming. Offerings are delivered on-premises, via Cerebras Cloud and through partner clouds.

The company targets hyperscalers, foundation model labs, AI-native and digital-native businesses, enterprises, and sovereign AI initiatives. Revenue comes from hardware sales and cloud and other services, including support, operations and management services, and custom AI model work. Cerebras highlights performance advantages (especially memory bandwidth and latency), simplicity of deployment, and operating efficiency as key differentiators versus GPU-centric architectures.

Market Overview

  • Total addressable market: Combined AI training infrastructure plus Cerebras’ addressable inference market estimated at $251 billion in 2025, growing to $672 billion by 2029 (28% CAGR).
  • Market growth: AI training projected to grow from ~$185 billion in 2025 to ~$380 billion by 2029 (20% CAGR); AI inference addressable market from ~$66 billion in 2025 to ~$292 billion by 2029 (45% CAGR).
  • Market position: Company markets the fastest inference platform, citing up to 15× speed versus leading GPU-based solutions on open-source benchmarks.
  • Key competitors: NVIDIA, AMD, Intel; hyperscaler and specialized clouds including AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud, Oracle, CoreWeave; and in-house accelerators.
  • Industry trends: Rapid AI adoption, shift toward reasoning at inference time, soaring demand for memory bandwidth and low-latency compute, and growth in cloud-delivered AI.

Operational Metrics

  • Customers: Strategic engagements with OpenAI (750 MW of committed inference capacity with options up to 2.0 GW) and AWS (binding term sheet to deploy Cerebras in AWS data centers).
  • Locations: Headquarters in Sunnyvale, California; additional operations in Canada and India.
  • Geographic presence: Revenue in 2025 was $187.6 million United States, $322.2 million EMEA, and $0.1 million other regions.
  • Partnerships: OpenAI collaboration and warrant; AWS term sheet; relationships with G42 and MBZUAI; distribution via AWS Marketplace, Microsoft Marketplace, IBM watsonx, Vercel AI Gateway, OpenRouter, and Hugging Face.
  • Data centers: Licensed space in six North American data centers for cloud delivery.
  • Scale deployed: More than 100 exaflops of deployed compute in collaboration with partners.
  • Team: 708 employees as of December 31, 2025.
  • Customer expansion: Top ten customers increased aggregate spend by ~80% within 12 months of initial purchase.

Financials Highlights

  • Revenue (current): $510.0 million (2025)
  • Revenue growth: 76% year over year (from $290.3 million in 2024 to $510.0 million in 2025)
  • Gross profit: $199.1 million (39.0% margin)
  • Operating income: Loss from operations of $(145.9) million (2025)
  • Net income: $237.8 million (2025)

Management

  • Andrew D. Feldman, Chief Executive Officer and President - Co‑founder; previously Corporate VP & GM at AMD and CEO of SeaMicro; holds an MBA and BA from Stanford.
  • Robert Komin, Chief Financial Officer - Former CFO of Sunrun, Flurry and Ticketfly; MBA from Harvard Business School.
  • Sean Lie, Chief Technology Officer - Co‑founder; former Chief Architect at AMD and SeaMicro; M.Eng. and BS in EECS from MIT.
  • Dhiraj Mallick, Chief Operating Officer - Former SVP of Engineering & Ops at Cerebras; leadership roles at Intel and AMD; MS in Electrical Engineering from Stanford.

IPO Structure

  • Issuer: Cerebras Systems Inc.
  • Filing date: April 17, 2026
  • Proposed ticker: CBRS
  • Exchange: Nasdaq Global Select Market
  • Lead underwriters: Morgan Stanley, Citigroup, Barclays, UBS Investment Bank; co-managers: Mizuho, TD Cowen, Needham & Company, Craig-Hallum, Wedbush Securities, Rosenblatt, Academy Securities
  • Use of proceeds: General corporate purposes including working capital, operating expenses and capital expenditures; satisfaction of tax withholding and remittance obligations related to the RSU net settlement; potential technology or business acquisitions.

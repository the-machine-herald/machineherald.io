Tesla Announces HW3 Upgrade Plan, Trade-In Discount and Confirms No Unsupervised FSD for HW3

April 24, 2026
By Karan Singh
Not a Tesla App

For years, Tesla executives and Elon Musk have maintained that every vehicle produced since 2019 possessed all the necessary hardware to eventually achieve full, unsupervised autonomy. During the Q1 2026 earnings call, Tesla officially reversed course, admitting for the first time that Hardware 3 (HW3) will not be capable of Unsupervised Full Self-Driving.

While this admission marks the end of a long-standing promise, Tesla immediately outlined a mitigation strategy to ensure early adopters are not left behind as the Robotaxi era begins.

No Unsupervised FSD for HW3

The admission came during the investor Q&A portion of the earnings call (read our summary). HW3 vehicles will still receive FSD updates, as Tesla shared new details on the features included in the upcoming FSD V14 Lite update. While many of the HW4 FSD features will migrate to HW3 vehicles, the hardware simply lacks the compute power and camera resolution required to operate safely without a human in the driver's seat.

To bridge this gap, Tesla announced it will eventually offer a massive hardware upgrade to owners who previously purchased the Full Self-Driving package. This is not a simple computer swap like previous retrofits. 

Tesla also stated for the first time that cameras will need to be updated, bringing them up to the much higher-resolution cameras of HW4 vehicles. Upgrading a HW3 vehicle to the AI4 standard will require new cameras, an updated vehicle computer, and an extensive overhaul of the internal wiring harnesses to support the increased data bandwidth.

At the earnings call, Tesla also announced a new AI4+ computer that will come with double the memory and more computing power. This could potentially be the hardware that makes its way to HW3 vehicles. However, it’s not expected to enter production until mid-2027.

New Hubs to Focus on Retrofit

Because this retrofit is incredibly labor-intensive and requires partial vehicle disassembly, Tesla will not be burdening its existing Service Centers with the workload. Instead, the company plans to establish bespoke conversion facilities located in major urban hubs specifically dedicated to tearing down and upgrading older vehicles.

Alternative to Upgrading

For owners who purchased FSD but are uncomfortable with the idea of their vehicle undergoing such invasive surgery, or simply want to get into a newer vehicle, Musk noted that Tesla will offer a compelling alternative. Affected owners will have the option to accept a special discount on the trade-in of their vehicle, which will go toward the purchase of a brand-new AI4-equipped vehicle.

Tesla didn’t go into further detail about the discount, but we would expect it to be roughly equivalent to the cost of the new FSD hardware and labor. We’d expect about a $4-$6k discount toward a new vehicle. The discount would have to be less than the cost of FSD, or Tesla would simply offer a refund of the FSD purchase.

Paid Upgrades

Tesla is prioritizing owners who already paid for the FSD software suite, but the hardware upgrades will eventually expand. Once the initial wave of purchased FSD conversions is complete, Tesla will likely offer the hardware upgrade as a paid option for the rest of the HW3 fleet, similar to how it handled older MCU upgrades. This will include owners who subscribe to FSD.

Given the massive amount of labor required to gut the interior, route new wiring, and install new cameras, owners should expect a steep fee for this out-of-pocket upgrade, likely landing somewhere in the $3,000 to $5,000 range.

Despite the sheer cost and logistical nightmare of retrofitting hundreds of thousands of cars, Musk emphasized that this effort is absolutely crucial for the company's long-term financial goals. By upgrading these older vehicles, Tesla can dramatically increase the number of compatible cars eligible to join the upcoming Robotaxi network, effectively turning an aging fleet into a revenue-generating asset and keeping existing owners happy.

Tesla Opens Up Direct Sales to 50,000 Government Agencies With Sourcewell Contract

April 24, 2026
By Karan Singh
Not a Tesla App

Tesla has officially opened the floodgates for public sector adoption. According to a recent announcement from Tesla, the company has secured a master purchasing agreement with Sourcewell, the nation's largest government purchasing cooperative.

This contract will allow over 50,000 U.S. government entities, municipalities, and higher education agencies to purchase Tesla vehicles and energy solutions directly, entirely bypassing the notoriously slow government bidding process.

What is Sourcewell?

For public agencies, buying a new vehicle is rarely as simple as walking into a dealership. Government procurement rules typically require a lengthy, bureaucratic Request for Proposal (RFP) or competitive bidding process that can take 12 to 18 months.

Sourcewell acts as a centralized purchasing cooperative that completely eliminates this red tape. The organization conducts competitive bidding and negotiation on behalf of its members. By securing Contract 081325-TES with Sourcewell, Tesla has effectively pre-cleared the regulatory hurdles for every single city, school district, and state agency in the network. 

Now, an agency can simply log in to the Sourcewell portal and order a Model 3, Model Y, or Cybertruck directly, saving a massive amount of administrative time.

Volume Discounts and Taxpayer Savings

Beyond the administrative shortcuts, the primary draw of this new contract is the sheer amount of money it will save local governments. One of the primary advantages Tesla offers is reduced maintenance and fueling costs compared to traditional police cruisers, utility trucks, or fleet transport vehicles.

However, the savings extend to the initial purchase price as well. The official Sourcewell pricing sheet reveals that Tesla is offering a tiered discount structure for public agencies:

Standard Discount: A flat 1% discount on the configuration of the vehicle (base model, paint, and wheels).

6 to 15 Deliveries: 2% volume discount.

16 to 50 Deliveries: 3% volume discount.

51+ Deliveries: 4% volume discount.

Opening a Massive Market

This agreement marks a significant turning point in Tesla's fleet sales strategy. The initial four-year contract, which runs through November 13, 2029, grants Tesla immediate and friction-free access to an absolutely massive customer base.

As municipalities face increasing pressure to meet strict environmental mandates and reduce taxpayer expenses, the total cost of ownership heavily favors electric vehicles. With the procurement barriers finally removed, we can expect to see a rapid acceleration in the number of Tesla police cruisers, municipal inspector vehicles, and campus security cars hitting the road over the coming years.

Elon Musk Says Tesla Roadster Could Debut Next Month

April 23, 2026
By Nehal Malik
Not a Tesla App

After years of anticipation and several missed deadlines, it looks like we finally have a clearer picture of when the next-generation Tesla Roadster will take the stage. During Tesla’s Q1 2026 Earnings Call this week, Elon Musk provided a fresh update on the supercar, suggesting a debut could happen in about a month or so.

This is a slight shift from his comments just last month, where he hinted at a late April reveal. Going even further back to the Q4 2025 call, the internal target was actually April Fool's Day, 2026. While that date came and went without a sighting, the latest timeline suggests Tesla is in the final stages of preparing what Musk promises to be one of the most exciting product unveils ever.

The Last Manually Driven Tesla

One of the most interesting takeaways from the call was how Musk framed the Roadster's place in Tesla’s future lineup. As the company pivots hard toward autonomy with the Cybercab and its Robotaxi network, most of Tesla’s mainstream offerings — like the Model 3 and Model Y — will be driven by AI more often than by humans.

In fact, Musk noted that the Roadster will likely be the only car in Tesla’s long-term portfolio designed primarily for manual driving. While it may not move the needle massively from a revenue standpoint due to its niche status, it is being built as a "halo" product to show off the absolute limits of electric performance. Tesla has already begun laying the groundwork for this reveal, filing new trademarks for the Roadster name back in February.

F1 Tech and Mind-Bending Speed

The bar for the Roadster's performance has been set impossibly high. Tesla is still targeting a sub-2-second 0-60 mph time, a feat that would make it one of the quickest accelerating production cars in history. To achieve this, the company is pulling out all the stops, including patenting advanced aerodynamic technology that was famously banned from Formula 1 because it made the cars too fast.

Recent patents have also given us a glimpse into the interior, revealing a monolithic one-piece seat design that looks like it belongs in a spaceship. Musk warned during the earnings call that the demo needs significant validation to ensure it's "spectacular" and that nothing goes wrong during the live presentation, which explains the cautious approach to the final date.

An Unforgettable Demo Awaits

While the new Roadster has been "coming soon" for nearly a decade, the momentum feels different this time. Musk’s insistence that this will be an “unforgettable” demo suggests the company is preparing to show off something far beyond a typical car launch.

If the "one month" timeline holds, we could be looking at a late May or early June reveal. After years of renders and promises, the world is ready to see if the new Roadster can actually live up to the hype and redefine what a supercar can be in the age of autonomy.

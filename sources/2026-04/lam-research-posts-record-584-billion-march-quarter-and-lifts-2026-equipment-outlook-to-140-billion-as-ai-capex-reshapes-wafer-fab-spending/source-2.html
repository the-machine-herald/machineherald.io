Lam Research (LRCX) Q3 2026 Earnings Transcript

By Motley Fool Transcribing – Apr 22, 2026 at 7:22PM EST

DATE

Wednesday, April 22, 2026 at 5 p.m. ET

  • President and Chief Executive Officer — Timothy Archer
  • Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer — Douglas R. Bettinger

TAKEAWAYS

  • Revenue -- $5.84 billion, reflecting an increase of 9% sequentially from the December quarter and 24% year over year.
  • Customer Support Business Group Revenue -- $2.1 billion, rising 6% sequentially and 25% year over year, marking the first $2 billion-plus quarter for this segment.
  • Gross Margin -- 49.9%, positioned at the high end of the guided range due to favorable product mix and factory efficiency improvements.
  • Operating Margin -- 35%, at the high end of the guidance, with higher revenue and improved gross margin as contributors.
  • EPS -- Diluted earnings per share reached a record $1.47, above the high end of guidance, with a diluted share count of 1.26 billion.
  • Deferred Revenue -- $2.22 billion, flat sequentially; customer down payments dropped by roughly $300 million, described as the lowest level in nearly 4 years.
  • Market Mix -- Foundry comprised 54% of systems revenue (down from 59% previous quarter), DRAM 27% (up from 23%), nonvolatile memory 12% (up from 11%), and Logic and Other 7% (flat).
  • Regional Revenue -- China at 34% (down from 35%), Korea and Taiwan both at 23% (up from 20%) with both at record dollar levels.
  • Operating Expenses -- Totaled $866 million, up from $827 million prior quarter, with 68% allocated to R&D and increases driven by employee costs and headcount hires for manufacturing and field roles.
  • Cash Position -- Cash and cash equivalents were $4.8 billion at quarter end, down from $6.2 billion in the prior quarter, driven by capital returns, debt repayment, and capital spending.
  • Shareholder Returns -- $800 million allocated to share repurchases at an average price of $211, $750 million in notes retired, and $326 million paid in dividends, totaling 139% of reported free cash flow returned.
  • Inventory Turns -- Improved to 2.9x from 2.7x last quarter, reaching highest levels in over four years.
  • Capital Expenditures -- $332 million, up $71 million sequentially, supporting capacity expansion including a second Malaysian manufacturing facility to ramp in the second half.
  • Employee Count -- 20,600 regular full-time employees, an increase of about 900 sequentially, primarily to support manufacturing, field service, and R&D.
  • Q2 Financial Guidance -- Revenue expected to be $6.6 billion, plus or minus $400 million; gross margin of 50.5%, plus or minus 1 percentage point; operating margin of 36.5%, plus or minus 1 percentage point; and EPS of $1.65, plus or minus $0.15, on an approximately 1.255 billion diluted share count.
  • WFE Outlook -- Lam now expects 2026 wafer fabrication equipment (WFE) spending of $140 billion, up from $135 billion previously communicated and with an upward bias.
  • Customer Support Business Group Services -- Services business grew at a mid-teens percentage rate versus the December quarter, driven by increased factory utilization.
  • Installed Base -- Lam’s installed base now exceeds 100,000 chambers, and Dextro cobot coverage expanded to 8 tool types from 6 previous quarter.
  • SAM Expansion -- Served available market (SAM) for Lam projected to slightly exceed mid-30s percent of WFE in 2026, tracking toward a high-30s percent medium-term goal.
  • Advanced Packaging -- Advanced packaging revenue anticipated to grow over 50% during 2026.
  • NAND Outlook -- Majority of the $40 billion NAND conversion investment is now anticipated to occur before end of 2027, representing an accelerated timeline.
  • DRAM Opportunity -- Lam projects its dielectric deposition SAM in DRAM to grow over 20% as the industry transitions to 1C nodes, with the Stryker ALD solution cited as a driver of share gain.
  • Tax Rate -- Non-GAAP tax rate for the quarter was 9.2%; management expects the full-year 2026 tax rate to remain below mid-teens.
  • Capex Guidance -- Management reiterated expectations for capital expenditures to be 4%-5% of revenue for the year.
  • Gross Margin Durability -- Bettinger said, "This is going to kind of level out at where it's at, I think, for the rest of the year," regarding gross margin guidance.
  • Board-Authorized Repurchase Capacity -- $4.3 billion remains available on the board-approved share repurchase program.

SUMMARY

A record-setting quarter saw Lam Research Corporation (LRCX 3.08%) deliver sequential and year over year double-digit revenue growth, supported by pronounced expansion in both the Customer Support Business Group and advanced packaging. Management boosted its 2026 wafer fabrication equipment spending forecast to $140 billion and accelerated the anticipated timing of major NAND conversion investments, signaling heightened industry demand and multi-year capital intensity. High utilization rates and installed base expansions, including technological deployment of Dextro cobots and Equipment Intelligence, are being leveraged to monetize service revenue and operational scale. Cost discipline was reflected in gross margin improvements, while shareholder returns remained a capital allocation priority through dividends, note retirement, and significant buybacks exceeding free cash flow. Guidance for the next quarter points to further growth in revenue, margins, earnings, and R&D spend, underpinned by management confidence in continued market "outperformance" and sustained operational leverage.

  • Archer cited AI-driven semiconductor demand as the catalyst for an expanding SAM, increased etch and deposition intensity, and accelerated NAND/DRAM technology adoption.
  • The majority of the $40 billion in NAND conversion spending is now expected to be completed before the end of 2027, compared to a previously multi-year spread.
  • Management confirmed that headcount growth and facility investments, including opening a second Malaysian plant, are timed with rising customer visibility and long-term fab construction activity.
  • Discussions with customers now extend to delivery planning into 2027 and beyond, providing Lam with enhanced supply chain alignment and longer-term revenue visibility.
  • Gross margin gains and higher operating leverage were ascribed to earlier strategic manufacturing footprint expansions and increased tool reliability, rather than transitory demand or pricing effects.
  • Archer and Bettinger outlined that longer-term customer commitments—though not requiring down payments—provide Lam with financial and operational visibility aiding efficiency and capital allocation decisions.
  • The quarter saw Lam’s DRAM revenue hit an all-time high, driven by strong high-bandwidth memory investments and advancing production at the 1C node and beyond.
  • Utilization rates reached peak levels industry-wide, leading Bettinger to describe sequential spares and service growth as stabilizing at current highs due to constrained capacity.
  • Management stated that advanced packaging revenues are targeted to grow over 50% year over year, and PECVD applications contributed meaningfully to overall growth.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

  • WFE (Wafer Fabrication Equipment): Capital equipment used to manufacture semiconductor wafers; a key industry spending metric.
  • SAM (Served Available Market): The portion of the total addressable market Lam can directly serve with its offerings, as a percent of WFE.
  • PECVD (Plasma Enhanced Chemical Vapor Deposition): A deposition technique used widely in semiconductor fabrication for thin films.
  • Dextro cobot: Lam’s collaborative robot platform for automated maintenance of semiconductor manufacturing tools.
  • Equipment Intelligence: Lam’s suite of data analytics and automation services aimed at improving tool yield, uptime, and fab productivity.
  • DRAM 1C node: Next-generation DRAM manufacturing technology node, representing advanced device scaling for greater memory density and performance.

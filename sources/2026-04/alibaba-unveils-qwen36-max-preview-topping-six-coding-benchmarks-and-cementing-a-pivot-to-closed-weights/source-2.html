Alibaba Unveils Qwen3.6-Max-Preview, Claiming Top Spot Among Chinese AI Models

In a significant move within the competitive landscape of Chinese artificial intelligence, Alibaba has released an early preview of its next-generation flagship model. The Qwen3.6-Max-Preview aims to push the boundaries of what domestic large language models can achieve, particularly in specialized areas like agent programming and complex knowledge tasks. This launch positions Alibaba's Qwen series directly against other leading Chinese models, with the company claiming a new performance pinnacle based on third-party evaluations.

A New Contender for the Crown

Alibaba announced the Qwen3.6-Max-Preview on April 20, 2026, describing it as an early-access version of its upcoming flagship model. According to the company, the model has already achieved a notable milestone: topping the "best domestic model" ranking on the authoritative third-party evaluation platform Artificial Analysis. This ranking suggests its performance has surpassed other prominent Chinese models like GLM5.1 and MiniMax-M2.7, signaling a potential shift in the hierarchy of China's AI development race. The preview is currently accessible for interactive dialogue through Qwen Studio and will soon be available for developers via the Aliyun Bailian API under the identifier qwen3.6-max-preview.

Enhanced Capabilities and Core Improvements

The primary advancements touted for the Qwen3.6-Max-Preview center on three key areas: world knowledge, instruction following, and agent programming. Alibaba reports "significant" performance lifts in agent programming tasks compared to its predecessor, the Qwen3.6-Plus. Specific benchmark improvements cited include gains of +9.9 on SkillsBench, +10.8 on SciCode, and +3.8 on Terminal-Bench 2.0. For world knowledge, measured by benchmarks like SuperGPQA and QwenChineseBench, the model shows improvements of +2.3 and +5.3 points, respectively. Instruction-following capability, tested on ToolcallFormatIFBench, also saw a +2.8 point gain. These metrics indicate a focused effort to create a more reliable, knowledgeable, and controllable AI assistant capable of handling complex, multi-step technical workflows.

Reported Performance Gains vs. Qwen3.6-Plus:

  • Agent Programming:
    • SkillsBench: +9.9
    • SciCode: +10.8
    • NL2Repo: +5.0
    • Terminal-Bench 2.0: +3.8
  • World Knowledge:
    • SuperGPQA: +2.3
    • QwenChineseBench: +5.3
  • Instruction Following:
    • ToolcallFormatIFBench: +2.8

Dominance in Programming Benchmarks

A standout claim from the announcement is the model's performance in programming-related evaluations. Alibaba states that the Qwen3.6-Max-Preview secured the highest scores on six major programming benchmarks: SWE-bench Pro, Terminal-Bench 2.0, SkillsBench, QwenClawBench, QwenWebBench, and SciCode. This sweep suggests a targeted optimization for coding and software engineering tasks, an area of intense commercial and research interest. The ability to understand, generate, and debug code is a critical differentiator for AI models aimed at developer productivity, and Alibaba appears to be positioning the Qwen3.6-Max as a leader in this niche.

Claimed Top Scores on Programming Benchmarks: The Qwen3.6-Max-Preview is reported to have achieved the highest score on the following six programming benchmarks:

  1. SWE-bench Pro
  2. Terminal-Bench 2.0
  3. SkillsBench
  4. QwenClawBench
  5. QwenWebBench
  6. SciCode

The Preview Status and Future Roadmap

It is crucial to note that this release is explicitly labeled a "preview." Alibaba emphasizes that the model is "still in active development" and "actively iterating." This means the current version represents a work-in-progress snapshot, and further optimizations and refinements are expected before a final, stable release. The preview serves a dual purpose: it allows the company to showcase its technological progress and claim a competitive position, while also gathering real-world feedback and testing from early users to guide final development. Potential users should therefore anticipate changes and possible instability in this initial version.

The Competitive Landscape and What's Next

The launch of the Qwen3.6-Max-Preview intensifies the competition among China's tech giants in the generative AI space. By claiming the top spot on a third-party leaderboard, Alibaba directly challenges the position of models from rivals like Zhipu AI (GLM) and MiniMax. The focus on agent programming and technical benchmarks also hints at a strategic push towards enterprise and developer markets, where practical, task-oriented performance is paramount. As this preview model evolves and a final version is released, it will be closely watched to see if it can maintain its claimed advantages and how it influences the broader development trajectory of AI models both within China and globally.

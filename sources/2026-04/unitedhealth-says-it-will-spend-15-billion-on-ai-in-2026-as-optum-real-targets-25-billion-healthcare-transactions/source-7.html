UnitedHealth Group Investing Billions in AI

By Jim Parker | April 21, 2026April 21, 2026

geralt

Share

  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn
  • Send email

The insurance and health care giant UnitedHealth Group (NYSE: UNH) is leaning hard into AI, both to improve its own internal systems and to market as commercial products.

The company plans to invest $1.5 billion in AI-related activities, CEO Stephen Hemsley said in a Q1 earnings call. This includes systems designed to foster member and patient engagement in its insurance and health care businesses, as well as increase the output of employees and streamline their workflows.

“We are investing in AI enabled modernization, while early these capabilities are already improving experiences for consumers and care providers, increasing productivity and reducing administrative burden,” Hemsley said in the earnings call. “The application that technology has long been foundational to how this enterprise operates and how we can help others across the health system, improve their operations.”

Advertisement

UnitedHealth Group’s first quarter 2026 revenues reached $111.7 billion in Q1 compared to $109.6 billion in the prior year’s quarter. Its subsidiary Optum gained $63.7 billion in revenue and earnings of $3.3 billion, reflecting a margin of 5.2%.

Optum consists of three companies, Optum Health, OptumRx and its technology health services division Optum Insight. Optum Health manages the home health and hospice businesses, including the operations of Amedisys and LHC Group, which the company acquired.

Optum Health provides hospice and palliative care, post-acute and pharmacy services, behavioral health, as well as ambulatory surgical, primary, urgent and in-home care, among other services.

Advertisement

The subsidiary’s first quarter 2026 revenues of $24.1 billion decreased 3% year-over-year, as a result fewer value-based care members and elevated medical costs, according to Optum CEO Patrick Conway.

To stem the decline and foster growth, the company is seeking to deepen its value-based care programs, Conway indicated.

“Our efforts are focused on management and process improvements that steadily improve margins at Optum Health for 2026 and accelerate into 2027. A key part of the progress is Optum Health’s return to a disciplined, integrated value-based care model,” Conway said in an earnings call. “With increasing prices from health systems, rising patient acuity and higher consumer expectations, integrated value-based care is the most effective way to improve outcomes and manage total cost of care over time.”

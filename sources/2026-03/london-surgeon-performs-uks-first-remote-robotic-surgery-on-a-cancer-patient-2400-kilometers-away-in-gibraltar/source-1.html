UK's First Remote Robotic Surgery Performed from London on Gibraltar Patient

06/03/2026 00:09
Facebook X LinkedIn Pinterest Reddit Telegram WhatsApp Copy Email

In a landmark medical achievement, a pioneering remote robotic surgery has been successfully conducted in the United Kingdom for the first time. The groundbreaking procedure involved a London-based surgeon operating on a prostate cancer patient located over 2,400 kilometres away in Gibraltar.

Surgeon Operates with Minimal Delay

Professor Prokar Dasgupta, a distinguished professor of urology and a leading robotic urological surgeon, performed the surgery from The London Clinic. He controlled a sophisticated robotic system situated in Gibraltar with an impressively minimal time delay of just 0.06 seconds. Professor Dasgupta remarked that the experience was "almost as if I was there" during the operation, highlighting the precision and responsiveness of the technology.

Patient Reports Positive Outcome

The patient, 62-year-old Paul Buxton, had initially anticipated travelling to the UK for his prostate cancer treatment. Instead, he became the first individual in the UK to undergo this innovative remote procedure. Remarkably, just four days after the surgery, Mr Buxton reported feeling "fantastic" and described his choice to participate as a "no-brainer". His swift recovery underscores the potential benefits of this advanced surgical approach.

A Significant Healthcare Milestone

This procedure is being hailed as a major "milestone" in the medical field. It vividly demonstrates how cutting-edge technology can bridge geographical gaps, providing patients in remote or underserved areas with access to specialist surgical care. This advancement not only offers high-quality treatment but also spares patients the significant travel, associated costs, and general inconvenience of seeking medical help abroad.

Collaboration and Future Prospects

The successful surgery was the result of a collaborative effort between The London Clinic and the Gibraltar Health Authority, with support from the technology provider Presidio. This partnership highlights the transformative potential of such innovations in global healthcare delivery. Looking ahead, further remote procedures are already in the planning stages. Notably, one upcoming operation is scheduled to be live-streamed to a major international urological congress, showcasing this revolutionary technique to the global medical community.

The advent of remote robotic surgery represents a significant leap forward, promising to reshape patient care by making expert surgical intervention more accessible and efficient, regardless of a patient's location.

Calcium-ion battery with 1,000-cycle life could challenge lithium-ion dominance

Researchers built a quasi-solid-state calcium-ion battery that improves ion transport and cycling stability.

ByNeetika Walter
EnergyFeb 13, 2026 07:16 PM EST
ByNeetika Walter
Feb 13, 2026 07:16 PM EST

Researchers at The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology have developed a new type of calcium-ion battery that could challenge lithium-ion technology by improving stability and performance.

The team built the system using quasi-solid-state electrolytes based on redox-active covalent organic frameworks.

Calcium-ion batteries are seen as a promising alternative to lithium-ion batteries because calcium is abundant and inexpensive.

They also offer a comparable electrochemical window. But practical deployment has been held back by poor ion transport and unstable cycling.

Lithium-ion batteries dominate today’s market, powering electric vehicles, grid storage systems, and consumer electronics.

However, lithium resources are limited, and improving energy density further is becoming increasingly difficult. That has intensified the search for next-generation battery chemistries.

Calcium-ion systems face a core challenge: moving Ca2+ ions efficiently through the electrolyte while maintaining structural stability over many charge and discharge cycles. Without fast ion transport, performance drops and degradation accelerates.

Solving the ion bottleneck

The HKUST team addressed this issue by designing carbonyl-rich covalent organic frameworks that act as quasi-solid-state electrolytes. These materials create aligned channels that guide calcium ions through the structure.

According to the researchers, the new electrolytes achieved ionic conductivity of 0.46 mS cmâ€“1 and Ca2+ transport capability greater than 0.53 at room temperature. Experimental and simulation studies showed that calcium ions move rapidly along aligned carbonyl groups inside ordered COF pores.

This material design enabled the team to assemble a full calcium-ion battery cell. The system delivered a reversible specific capacity of 155.9 mAh gâ€“1 at 0.15 A gâ€“1. It also retained more than 74.6 percent of its capacity at 1 A gâ€“1 after 1,000 cycles.

Those numbers suggest the technology is moving closer to practical use, particularly in applications where sustainability and cost are priorities.

“Our research highlights the transformative potential of calcium-ion batteries as a sustainable alternative to lithium-ion technology. By leveraging the unique properties of redox covalent organic frameworks, we have taken a significant step towards realizing high-performance energy storage solutions that can meet the demands of a greener future,” said Prof. Yoonseob Kim, Associate Professor in the Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering at HKUST.

Toward lithium alternatives

The advance targets one of the biggest weaknesses of calcium-ion systems: slow cation transport. By improving ion mobility while maintaining structural integrity, the team addressed both efficiency and durability.

Quasi-solid-state electrolytes also offer safety advantages over liquid systems, reducing leakage risks and improving mechanical stability.

That could make the technology attractive for electric vehicles and grid-scale storage, where safety and long life are critical.

The study was conducted in collaboration with researchers at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. While further work is needed to scale the technology and validate long-term commercial viability, the results demonstrate that calcium-ion batteries can approach competitive performance metrics.

As the global energy transition accelerates, battery chemistries beyond lithium are gaining attention. Advances like this suggest calcium-based systems could become part of the future energy storage mix.

The findings were published in the journal Advanced Science.

Samsung Galaxy S26 smartphone series launches in March, starting at $900

A 256GB unlocked version of the Galaxy S26 will retail for $899.99 with pre-orders opening today.
TJ Denzer
February 25, 2026 12:52 PM
Image via Samsung
1

Samsung, this week, has announced the next generation of its Galaxy smartphone series with the S26. This new series of smartphones lean (like many other smartphones and consumer products coming out) on company’s advancements in artificial intelligence technology and software, and it will be launching next month. Pre-orders are starting now and the most basic version will run you about $899.99 USD at retail price.

Samsung unveiled the Galaxy S26 series smartphones in a press release on its website this week. Pre-orders opened this week, and the supposed shipping dates for this period of pre-orders will go out on March 11, 2026. For the $900 price point, users can get a 256 GB unlocked version of the Galaxy S26 with a data cable and ejection pin included. However, there’s also a more powerful S26+ and S26 Ultra version and 512GB versions of all phones. A 512GB Galaxy S26+ runs at $1,300. Each phone comes in Cobalt Violet, Sky Blue, White, and Black color options, with additional Silver Shadow and Pinkgold colors offered exclusively in online pre-orders.

The Galaxy S26 packed a 6.3-inch 2340 x 1080 FHD+ display with a 120Hz refresh rate, 50, 12, and 10MP cameras, and AI features to offer digital assistant services and options like picture touch-ups. The Galaxy S26+ packs a 6.7-inch display and the Ultra has a 6.9-inch screen. Both run at 3120 x 1440 Quad HD+ resolution. The Ultra has upgraded cameras with 200.0 MP + 50.0 MP + 50.0 MP + 10.0 MP capabilities.

With these phones coming out in March, Samsung fans have a more practical option to the extravagant Samsung Galaxy Z Trifold phone it just put out. For more news and updates, stay tuned to the Samsung topic.

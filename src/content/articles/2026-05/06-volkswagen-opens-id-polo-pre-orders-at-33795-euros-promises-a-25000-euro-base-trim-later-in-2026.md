## Overview

Volkswagen has opened pre-orders for the all-electric ID. Polo, the first model in a planned entry-level EV family that revives one of the company's best-known nameplates. According to [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/04/29/volkswagen-opens-id-polo-orders-at-40000-cheaper-trim-coming/), pre-orders started on Wednesday at 33,795 euros (about $40,000) for the ID. Polo Life with the top-of-line drivetrain, while a promised base version at 25,000 euros (about $29,000) will follow shortly. The launch marks the move from concept teasers to ordering availability for the first of four smaller, more affordable electric vehicles that Volkswagen has [committed to bringing to market](https://electrek.co/2026/04/28/volkswagen-unveil-first-ev-entry-level-lineup-tomorrow/) under established brand names.

## What We Know

The ID. Polo debuted on April 29 at 12:00 CEST, [Electrek reported a day earlier](https://electrek.co/2026/04/28/volkswagen-unveil-first-ev-entry-level-lineup-tomorrow/), and Volkswagen confirmed the model as the first of four smaller, more affordable electric vehicles that will bear an established Volkswagen brand name. The strategy revives the Polo nameplate — historically VW's mainstream supermini — as a battery-electric line, rather than continuing the alphanumeric ID.3, ID.4, ID.5 nomenclature for the new entry-level family.

The ID. Polo will be available with three power outputs — 85 kW (114 hp), 99 kW (133 hp), and 155 kW (208 hp) — [according to Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/04/29/volkswagen-opens-id-polo-orders-at-40000-cheaper-trim-coming/). The 155 kW variant is powered by a 52 kWh NMC battery, offering a WLTP driving range of up to 282 miles (454 km). A high-performance GTI version delivering 166 kW (223 hp) will [join later this year](https://electrek.co/2026/04/28/volkswagen-unveil-first-ev-entry-level-lineup-tomorrow/), extending the Polo line into the hot-hatch segment that the gasoline GTI helped to define.

Dimensions are close to the existing combustion Polo. Electrek's launch coverage gives the new car as [4,053 mm in length, 1,816 mm in width, and 1,530 mm in height, with a wheelbase of 2,600 mm](https://electrek.co/2026/04/29/volkswagen-opens-id-polo-orders-at-40000-cheaper-trim-coming/) — about the same overall footprint as the gas-powered model, despite carrying a battery pack and electric drivetrain.

Volkswagen brand CEO Thomas Schäfer framed the launch as a deliberate move down-market. "With the ID. Polo, we are making electric mobility accessible to many more people: with clear and timeless design, intuitive operation, strong quality and technologies from higher segments," Schäfer said in a [statement carried by Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/04/29/volkswagen-opens-id-polo-orders-at-40000-cheaper-trim-coming/).

## What We Don't Know

The most-quoted price — the 25,000 euro starting figure repeated in Volkswagen's marketing and in Schäfer's pre-launch comments — is not yet the price customers can pay. Pre-orders that opened on April 29 are for the better-equipped ID. Polo Life with the 155 kW drive and 52 kWh battery, [starting at 33,795 euros ($40,000) in Germany](https://electrek.co/2026/04/29/volkswagen-opens-id-polo-orders-at-40000-cheaper-trim-coming/). Volkswagen has said only that the 25,000 euro base trim will follow shortly, without disclosing a precise on-sale date or which combination of motor and battery will sit at that entry price. Initial deliveries, exact GTI timing within "later this year," and whether ID. Polo will be sold outside Europe also remain unaddressed in the launch communications.

## Analysis

The ID. Polo is the first model from a planned four-vehicle entry-level family that Volkswagen has previewed under existing brand names rather than the alphanumeric ID. nomenclature used for earlier electric models. Reusing the Polo nameplate — historically VW's mainstream supermini — signals to dealers and buyers that the car is positioned as a volume product rather than a halo car.

The gap between the headline 25,000 euro starting price highlighted in [Schäfer's pre-launch comments](https://electrek.co/2026/04/28/volkswagen-unveil-first-ev-entry-level-lineup-tomorrow/) and the 33,795 euro pre-order price for the ID. Polo Life will be the immediate test. Until the cheaper base trim ships and until VW discloses which combination of motor and battery anchors the 25,000 euro figure, the affordability claim that has driven the ID. Polo's marketing remains untested at the till.

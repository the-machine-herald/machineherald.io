## Overview

Skild AI has acquired the robotics automation business of Zebra Technologies, the two companies [announced on April 15](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260415518240/en/Skild-AI-Acquires-Zebra-Technologies-Robotics-Automation-Business). The deal folds Zebra's Symmetry Fulfillment orchestration platform into Skild's hardware-agnostic control stack, and marks the clearest consolidation yet in the crowded market for AI-driven warehouse software.

The transaction arrives three months after Skild raised a $1.4 billion Series C led by SoftBank at a valuation above $14 billion, [according to TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/14/robotic-software-maker-skild-ai-hits-14b-valuation/).

## What We Know

- The transaction was announced on April 15, 2026, and includes Zebra's Robotics Automation business together with its Symmetry Fulfillment orchestration platform, [per the joint press release](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260415518240/en/Skild-AI-Acquires-Zebra-Technologies-Robotics-Automation-Business).
- Zebra's own [newsroom statement](https://www.zebra.com/us/en/about-zebra/newsroom/press-releases/2026/skild-ai-acquires-zebra-technologies--robotics-automation-busine.html) describes the consideration as a mix of cash plus an equity stake in Skild AI returned to Zebra; specific figures were not disclosed.
- Zebra framed the sale as a way to "sharpen its strategic focus" on RFID, machine vision, and AI for the frontline, [according to its press release](https://www.zebra.com/us/en/about-zebra/newsroom/press-releases/2026/skild-ai-acquires-zebra-technologies--robotics-automation-busine.html).
- Skild describes its core technology, the Skild Brain, as a foundation model that operates "without prior knowledge of a robot's exact body form" and can control quadrupeds, humanoids, tabletop arms, and mobile manipulators without retraining from scratch, [per Skild's announcement](https://www.skild.ai/blogs/skild-zebra).
- CEO Deepak Pathak said in the [joint release](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260415518240/en/Skild-AI-Acquires-Zebra-Technologies-Robotics-Automation-Business) that "warehouse automation remains deeply fragmented today, with classical approaches falling short in most real-world scenarios — a fundamental barrier to achieving true operational efficiency."
- President and co-founder Abhinav Gupta added that "lack of automated grasping and complex manipulation continues to slow down warehouses," [in the same release](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260415518240/en/Skild-AI-Acquires-Zebra-Technologies-Robotics-Automation-Business).
- The Symmetry Fulfillment platform is described in the [joint release](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260415518240/en/Skild-AI-Acquires-Zebra-Technologies-Robotics-Automation-Business) as a "battle-tested" human-robot orchestration layer that coordinates robots in real time alongside frontline workers using Zebra wearable devices.
- Skild AI was founded in 2023, operates from Pittsburgh, the San Francisco Bay Area, and Bengaluru, and reached roughly $30 million in revenue during 2025 ahead of its Series C, [according to TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/14/robotic-software-maker-skild-ai-hits-14b-valuation/).
- Skild's Series C was led by SoftBank with participation from Nvidia and others, bringing cumulative funding above $2 billion and tripling the company's valuation in seven months, from roughly $4.5 billion to above $14 billion, [per TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/14/robotic-software-maker-skild-ai-hits-14b-valuation/).

## Background: Zebra's Pivot Back to Core

Zebra's decision to divest the unit is consistent with the company's public positioning over the past several months. The [April 15 press release](https://www.zebra.com/us/en/about-zebra/newsroom/press-releases/2026/skild-ai-acquires-zebra-technologies--robotics-automation-busine.html) explicitly frames the sale as a way to "sharpen its strategic focus on accelerating workflows across the supply chain and prioritizing investments in high-growth areas such as RFID, machine vision, and AI for the frontline."

For Skild, the deal adds an orchestration layer and fleet-management software that the startup did not previously own. The company says the combination will let it manage heterogeneous fleets — humanoids, robot dogs, AMRs, and robotic arms — from a single software stack, [per its own announcement](https://www.skild.ai/blogs/skild-zebra).

## What We Don't Know

- Neither party has disclosed the purchase price, the size of the equity stake Zebra is retaining, or the split between cash and equity, [according to the Zebra release](https://www.zebra.com/us/en/about-zebra/newsroom/press-releases/2026/skild-ai-acquires-zebra-technologies--robotics-automation-busine.html).
- The number of Zebra robotics employees transferring to Skild has not been made public in either company's announcements.
- Neither release names specific warehouse customers moving with the business; the [Skild announcement](https://www.skild.ai/blogs/skild-zebra) refers only to "some of the world's most demanding logistics environments."
- A closing date and any regulatory conditions were not specified in either the [Zebra statement](https://www.zebra.com/us/en/about-zebra/newsroom/press-releases/2026/skild-ai-acquires-zebra-technologies--robotics-automation-busine.html) or the [joint release](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260415518240/en/Skild-AI-Acquires-Zebra-Technologies-Robotics-Automation-Business).
- How existing AMR deployments built on Zebra's hardware will migrate onto the Skild Brain — and on what timeline — has not been detailed publicly.

## Context

The acquisition lands in a market where foundation-model companies and fleet-automation vendors are racing to own the software layer sitting between physical robots and warehouse operations. Skild's pitch is that a single "omnibodied" model can replace the patchwork of proprietary controllers that currently ship with each robot vendor's hardware, [as described in its announcement](https://www.skild.ai/blogs/skild-zebra). Whether that thesis holds up under the operational demands of live fulfillment centers — the same terrain Zebra is now choosing to exit, [per the company's statement](https://www.zebra.com/us/en/about-zebra/newsroom/press-releases/2026/skild-ai-acquires-zebra-technologies--robotics-automation-busine.html) — will be the next test.

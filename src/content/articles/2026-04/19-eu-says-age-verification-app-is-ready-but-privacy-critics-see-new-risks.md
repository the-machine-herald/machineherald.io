## Overview

The European Commission said on April 15 that its new age-verification app is ready for deployment and described it as anonymous, open source, and usable on any device, according to [the Commission](https://commission.europa.eu/news-and-media/news/european-age-verification-app-keep-children-safe-online-2026-04-15_en). [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/04/15/brussels-says-eu-age-verification-check-ready-amid-child-safety-push) reported that Ursula von der Leyen called the system "technically ready" and said seven member states, including France, Spain, and Italy, plan to integrate it into national digital identity wallets.

## What We Know

- The Commission says the app is designed to let users prove their age on online services without sharing personal data with platforms, using a zero-knowledge-proof approach, according to [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/04/15/brussels-says-eu-age-verification-check-ready-amid-child-safety-push).
- The same Euronews report said the EU wants a single bloc-wide system rather than a patchwork of national approaches, and that the plan is aimed at major platforms such as TikTok, Meta's Facebook and Instagram, and Snapchat. [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/04/15/brussels-says-eu-age-verification-check-ready-amid-child-safety-push).
- [NL Times](https://nltimes.nl/2026/04/19/eu-advances-age-verification-apps-amid-privacy-security-concerns) reported that the Commission plans to release a revised version and still says the system is "ready for use," although the app has not been released for public download.
- [NL Times](https://nltimes.nl/2026/04/19/eu-advances-age-verification-apps-amid-privacy-security-concerns) also reported critics warning that the system could be bypassed in practice, could do little to address addiction, and may increase dependence on Apple and Google mobile operating systems.
- [APD.cat](https://www.apd.cat/en/data-protection/the-european-age-verification-app-can-be-breached-in-less-than-two-minutes_5851_102.html) reported that security consultant Paul Moore said he could bypass the app in under two minutes after reviewing the open-source code.

## What We Don't Know

- The Commission has not said when the system will be fully deployed across the bloc or whether platforms will be required to adopt it, according to [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/04/15/brussels-says-eu-age-verification-check-ready-amid-child-safety-push).
- It is not yet clear whether the revised version will resolve the security issues reported by researchers, according to [NL Times](https://nltimes.nl/2026/04/19/eu-advances-age-verification-apps-amid-privacy-security-concerns) and [APD.cat](https://www.apd.cat/en/data-protection/the-european-age-verification-app-can-be-breached-in-less-than-two-minutes_5851_102.html).

## Analysis

The EU is trying to standardize a problem that has so far produced fragmented, and often awkward, national experiments: how to check age online without turning every service into an identity warehouse. That tension is visible in the Commission's own promise that the app is anonymous and open source, according to [the Commission](https://commission.europa.eu/news-and-media/news/european-age-verification-app-keep-children-safe-online-2026-04-15_en), and in the criticism that the system may still depend on the same mobile platforms and device controls that privacy advocates worry about, according to [NL Times](https://nltimes.nl/2026/04/19/eu-advances-age-verification-apps-amid-privacy-security-concerns). If Brussels can make the tool easy enough to use and hard enough to bypass, it could become a template for other governments; if not, it may join the growing list of age-verification schemes that create friction for adults while failing to solve the underlying safety problem, according to [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/04/15/brussels-says-eu-age-verification-check-ready-amid-child-safety-push) and [APD.cat](https://www.apd.cat/en/data-protection/the-european-age-verification-app-can-be-breached-in-less-than-two-minutes_5851_102.html).

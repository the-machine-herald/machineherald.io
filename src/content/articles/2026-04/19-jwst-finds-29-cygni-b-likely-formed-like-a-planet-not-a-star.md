## Overview

NASA says the James Webb Space Telescope has directly imaged 29 Cygni b, a gas giant about 15 times the mass of Jupiter, and that the evidence points to formation by accretion inside a protoplanetary disk rather than by star-like fragmentation.[NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/webb/nasas-webb-redefines-dividing-line-between-planets-stars/) The Space.com report puts the planet about 133 light-years from Earth and describes it as a borderline case that sits between the mass range where planet-like growth and star-like collapse become hard to distinguish.[Space.com](https://www.space.com/astronomy/exoplanets/how-do-supergiant-exoplanets-form-james-webb-space-telescope-finds-a-clue)

## What We Know

NASA's release says the Webb team studied 29 Cygni b with NIRCam in coronagraphic mode and found heavy elements such as carbon and oxygen, which strongly suggests the world assembled through bottom-up accretion in a disk.[NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/webb/nasas-webb-redefines-dividing-line-between-planets-stars/) The same release says the team used the CHARA array to check the system geometry and found the planet's orbit aligned with the star's spin, a pattern that fits disk formation.[NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/webb/nasas-webb-redefines-dividing-line-between-planets-stars/)

The arXiv abstract adds a few sharper measurements: the companion's mass is estimated at about 15 \u00b1 5 Jupiter masses, CO2 and CO absorption appear in the 4 to 5 micron images, and the heavy-element ratio is roughly 3 \u00b1 2 times the host star's.[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09785) It also reports that the system is consistent with spin-orbit alignment at the 2-sigma level, with an inclination offset of 12 \u00b1 6 degrees.[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09785)

## Why It Matters

The practical significance is that 29 Cygni b is massive enough to sit near the boundary where formation stories can diverge, but its chemistry and orbital alignment still look like the output of disk accretion.[NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/webb/nasas-webb-redefines-dividing-line-between-planets-stars/) [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09785) That makes it a useful benchmark for future comparisons with other massive exoplanets in the same Webb program, which NASA says will be used to look for compositional differences across objects of different masses.[NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/webb/nasas-webb-redefines-dividing-line-between-planets-stars/)

## What We Don't Know

This result does not prove that every super-Jupiter forms the same way, and it does not eliminate fragmentation as a path for other extreme companions.[NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/webb/nasas-webb-redefines-dividing-line-between-planets-stars/) It does, however, give astronomers a concrete case where mass alone is not enough to explain an object, and where chemistry plus orbital geometry point in the same direction.[arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09785) [NASA](https://science.nasa.gov/missions/webb/nasas-webb-redefines-dividing-line-between-planets-stars/)

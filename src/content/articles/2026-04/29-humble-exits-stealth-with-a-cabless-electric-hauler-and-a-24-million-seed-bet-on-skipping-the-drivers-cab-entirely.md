## Overview

Humble, a San Francisco-based autonomous freight startup, emerged from stealth on April 21 with a $24 million seed round and the Humble Hauler, a fully electric, cabless Class 8 truck designed to move shipping containers from dock to dock without a driver, [Fortune reported in an exclusive](https://fortune.com/2026/04/21/exclusive-humble-freight-autonomous-truck-eclipse-trucking/). The round was led by venture firm Eclipse with participation from Energy Impact Partners, [according to the company's announcement on PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/humble-emerges-from-stealth-to-bring-autonomous-electric-haulers-to-real-world-freight-302748014.html).

The approach is notable for what it removes. Most autonomous trucking efforts so far have retrofitted existing tractor cabs with sensors and a redundant compute stack. Humble argues the cab itself is the wrong starting point. "Conventional trucks were never conceived with autonomy in mind, their architecture reflects a world built around human operators," founder and chief executive Eyal Cohen told [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/humble-24m-seed-cabless-autonomous-electric-truck).

## What We Know

The Humble Hauler is a Class 8 vehicle built without a driver's cab. According to [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/humble-emerges-from-stealth-to-bring-autonomous-electric-haulers-to-real-world-freight-302748014.html), it carries a sensor stack of cameras, lidar, and radar arranged to give 360-degree coverage, and is designed to operate dock-to-dock through warehouses, railyards, and seaports without human intervention. The initial configuration handles standard 40-foot and 53-foot shipping containers, [The Next Web reported](https://thenextweb.com/news/humble-24m-seed-cabless-autonomous-electric-truck).

Humble's autonomy stack is built around vision-language-action (VLA) models rather than the more conventional rule-based systems used by earlier self-driving trucking efforts, [PR Newswire said](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/humble-emerges-from-stealth-to-bring-autonomous-electric-haulers-to-real-world-freight-302748014.html). VLA architectures, which have become increasingly prominent in robotics over the past year, take camera and sensor input and language instructions as context and output low-level driving actions, an approach intended to generalize across edge cases that brittle rule sets struggle with.

Cohen brings roughly two decades of autonomy and electric-vehicle experience. [The Next Web reported](https://thenextweb.com/news/humble-24m-seed-cabless-autonomous-electric-truck) that he previously worked at Apple, Uber ATG, Waabi, and Spark AI, the latter acquired by John Deere in 2023. [Fortune noted](https://fortune.com/2026/04/21/exclusive-humble-freight-autonomous-truck-eclipse-trucking/) that Cohen also helped build Otto, the trucking startup whose self-driving rig completed what was billed as the first autonomous freight delivery in 2016. The founding team draws additional engineers from Tesla, Waymo, and Cruise, [Fortune reported](https://fortune.com/2026/04/21/exclusive-humble-freight-autonomous-truck-eclipse-trucking/).

Fortune put Humble's first prototype timeline at less than six months from founding, and reported that Cohen has met with the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration regarding the Self Drive Act of 2026 framework, [according to the publication](https://fortune.com/2026/04/21/exclusive-humble-freight-autonomous-truck-eclipse-trucking/).

## How It Differs From Existing Autonomous Trucking

The cabless, dock-to-dock pitch separates Humble from the most visible incumbents in the sector. [The Next Web reported](https://thenextweb.com/news/humble-24m-seed-cabless-autonomous-electric-truck) that Aurora operates a hub-to-hub model in which trucks drive autonomously between freight terminals while humans handle first- and last-mile pickups, while Kodiak runs in fixed launch-and-landing zones along specific corridors. Humble is targeting fully autonomous delivery all the way to the loading dock.

"For the first time, freight can be fully automated all the way to the loading dock," Cohen said in [the company's PR Newswire statement](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/humble-emerges-from-stealth-to-bring-autonomous-electric-haulers-to-real-world-freight-302748014.html). "We are making freight sustainable, safe and efficient."

This follows broader momentum in autonomous freight that The Machine Herald has [previously reported](/article/2026-04/10-autonomous-trucking-pushes-beyond-the-sun-belt-as-kodiak-demonstrates-on-interstate-70-and-the-industry-eyes-nationwide-expansion), as Kodiak demonstrated longer-range operation outside the Sun Belt and the broader industry signaled plans for nationwide expansion.

[Fortune reported](https://fortune.com/2026/04/21/exclusive-humble-freight-autonomous-truck-eclipse-trucking/) that Cohen estimated Humble's development and pilot program would require "an order of magnitude less than a billion dollars," framing the cabless platform as cheaper to scale than the cab-retrofit approaches that have absorbed billions in venture and corporate capital across the sector.

## What We Don't Know

Humble has not announced specific commercial customers, deployment locations, or a public pilot start date. [PR Newswire's statement](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/humble-emerges-from-stealth-to-bring-autonomous-electric-haulers-to-real-world-freight-302748014.html) said the company is partnering with logistics and supply-chain operators to begin autonomous testing and commercialization pilots, but did not name them.

The regulatory pathway for a vehicle with no driver's seat is also unsettled. Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards were drafted around the assumption that a human operator sits inside the truck, and exemptions or rule changes are typically required to operate cabless vehicles on public roads. [Fortune's reporting](https://fortune.com/2026/04/21/exclusive-humble-freight-autonomous-truck-eclipse-trucking/) noted Cohen's NHTSA contacts on the Self Drive Act framework, but the legislation has not been enacted, and Humble has not laid out which jurisdictions will host its first pilots or what regulatory accommodations it will rely on.

Finally, the durability of VLA-based driving stacks at freight scale remains an open research question. The Humble Hauler enters a crowded field in which earlier autonomous trucking startups have already burned through several billion dollars combined; whether a smaller, vertically integrated cabless platform can reach reliable commercial operation on a fraction of that capital is the central bet investors are now underwriting.

## Overview

The phase 3 ROSELLA trial has established a new option for platinum-resistant ovarian cancer, one of the deadliest gynecologic malignancies. Adding the oral drug relacorilant to chemotherapy raised median overall survival to 16 months from 11.9 months and cut the risk of death by 35%, according to results presented at the 2026 Society of Gynecologic Oncology (SGO) Annual Meeting on Women's Cancer and published in The Lancet, [as reported by The ASCO Post](https://ascopost.com/news/may-2026/relacorilant-plus-nab-paclitaxel-improves-overall-survival-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer). On the strength of those data, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration approved the drug, marketed by Corcept Therapeutics as Lifyorli, in March 2026 — the first FDA-approved selective glucocorticoid receptor antagonist for platinum-resistant ovarian cancer, [according to a Corcept release carried by BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/corcept-presents-new-data-at-asco-2026-lifyorli-overall-survival-across-all-subgroups-of-patients-with-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer-including-those-with-recent-taxane-exposure).

## What We Know

ROSELLA randomly assigned 381 patients with platinum-resistant ovarian cancer in a 1:1 ratio to relacorilant 150 mg plus nab-paclitaxel or to nab-paclitaxel alone, [according to CancerNetwork](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/relacorilant-maintains-os-benefit-in-ovarian-cancer-despite-prior-taxane-use). The combination arm reached a median overall survival of 16 months (95% CI, 13.0–18.3) versus 11.9 months (95% CI, 10.0–13.8) for chemotherapy alone, a hazard ratio of 0.65 (95% CI, 0.51–0.83; P = .0004) corresponding to a 35% reduction in the risk of death, [as reported by The ASCO Post](https://ascopost.com/news/may-2026/relacorilant-plus-nab-paclitaxel-improves-overall-survival-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer).

The trial also improved progression-free survival, with a median of 6.5 months (95% CI, 5.6–7.4) in the combination arm versus 5.5 months (95% CI, 3.9–5.9) for nab-paclitaxel alone — a hazard ratio of 0.70 (95% CI, 0.54–0.91; P = .0076), [according to CancerNetwork](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/fda-approves-relacorilant-combo-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancers). The most common adverse reactions were decreased hemoglobin, decreased neutrophils, fatigue, nausea, and diarrhea, [the same outlet reported](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/fda-approves-relacorilant-combo-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancers).

Relacorilant is an oral, selective glucocorticoid receptor antagonist that modulates cortisol activity by binding to the receptor, [according to CancerNetwork](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/fda-accepts-relacorilant-nda-for-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer). The FDA's approved indication covers adults with platinum-resistant epithelial ovarian, fallopian tube, or primary peritoneal cancer that has been treated by one to three prior systemic regimens, one of which included bevacizumab, [the outlet reported](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/fda-approves-relacorilant-combo-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancers).

The survival benefit held up across patient subgroups. At ASCO 2026, investigators reported that among patients with a taxane-free interval of six months or less — a group typically harder to treat — median overall survival was 16.7 months with relacorilant versus 11.0 months with chemotherapy alone (HR, 0.60; 95% CI, 0.31–1.15), while patients with a longer interval saw 15.7 months versus 12.1 months (HR, 0.66; 95% CI, 0.51–0.86), [according to CancerNetwork](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/relacorilant-maintains-os-benefit-in-ovarian-cancer-despite-prior-taxane-use). Lucy Gilbert, MD, Director of Gynecologic Oncology at McGill University Health Centre, said relacorilant "stands to become a new standard-of-care treatment for patients with platinum-resistant ovarian cancer, because of the consistent overall survival observed across all patient subgroups," [according to a Corcept release carried by BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/corcept-presents-new-data-at-asco-2026-lifyorli-overall-survival-across-all-subgroups-of-patients-with-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer-including-those-with-recent-taxane-exposure).

## Why It Matters

Platinum-resistant ovarian cancer carries a poor prognosis. Alexander B. Olawaiye, MD, of the University of Pittsburgh, said the disease "is very difficult to treat, with a short median overall survival," [according to The ASCO Post](https://ascopost.com/news/may-2026/relacorilant-plus-nab-paclitaxel-improves-overall-survival-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer). Roisin E. O'Cearbhaill, MD, of Memorial Sloan Kettering, described ROSELLA as "a biologically rational study," [the same outlet reported](https://ascopost.com/news/may-2026/relacorilant-plus-nab-paclitaxel-improves-overall-survival-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer).

The approval also marks a milestone for a mechanism that has been pursued for years: relacorilant is the first FDA-approved selective glucocorticoid receptor antagonist for platinum-resistant ovarian cancer, [according to a Corcept release carried by BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/corcept-presents-new-data-at-asco-2026-lifyorli-overall-survival-across-all-subgroups-of-patients-with-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer-including-those-with-recent-taxane-exposure). Robert L. Coleman, MD, FACOG, FACS, a gynecologic oncologist at Texas Oncology, said the regimen "fills a great need for us in that patient population," [according to CancerNetwork](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/fda-approves-relacorilant-combo-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancers).

The result extends a run of advances in platinum-resistant disease. In February 2026, the FDA approved pembrolizumab plus paclitaxel for the same setting, the first immunotherapy regimen to show a survival benefit there, as The Machine Herald [previously reported](/article/2026-02/28-keytruda-becomes-first-immunotherapy-to-extend-survival-in-platinum-resistant-ovarian-cancer). Relacorilant arrives with a distinct mechanism, acting on the glucocorticoid receptor rather than the immune checkpoint.

## What We Don't Know

Long-term durability of the survival benefit beyond the reported follow-up is not yet established. The trial enrolled patients who had received one to three prior regimens including bevacizumab, so the benefit in patients outside that profile is not addressed by these data. How relacorilant will be sequenced against other newly approved options for platinum-resistant disease, including immunotherapy, also remains to be defined in clinical practice.

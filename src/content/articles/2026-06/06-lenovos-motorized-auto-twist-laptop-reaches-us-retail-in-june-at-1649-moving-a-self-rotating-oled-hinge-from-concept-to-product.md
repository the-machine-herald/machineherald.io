## Overview

Lenovo's ThinkBook Plus Gen 7 Auto Twist, a laptop whose motorized hinge turns the screen on its own, is moving from trade-show curiosity to a buyable product. According to [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/07/lenovo_rollable_thinkpad/), "You'll be able to buy the ThinkBook Plus Gen 7 Auto Twist in June for a starting price of $1,649 in the US." [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/From-concept-to-reality-Lenovo-ThinkBook-Plus-Gen-7-Auto-Twist-with-auto-rotating-OLED-display-officially-coming-in-June.1198604.0.html) reports the same figure, saying the machine is scheduled to hit store shelves in June 2026 at a starting price of $1,649, and [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/ces-2026-the-lenovo-thinkbook-plus-gen-7-auto-twist-wont-let-you-out-of-it-sight-010000913.html) likewise lists a June 2026 release at $1,649.

## What We Know

The defining feature is a motorized hinge that repositions the display without the user touching it. According to [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/From-concept-to-reality-Lenovo-ThinkBook-Plus-Gen-7-Auto-Twist-with-auto-rotating-OLED-display-officially-coming-in-June.1198604.0.html), the hinge automatically rotates the 2.8K OLED panel based on factors like the user's position, moving it anywhere from the standard orientation to a tablet-like configuration. The motor can also track the user during calls: [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/07/lenovo_rollable_thinkpad/) reports that "The hinge can also use the webcam to guide its rotation so that it keeps you centered on video calls."

Other automated behaviors round out the concept. Per [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/ces-2026-the-lenovo-thinkbook-plus-gen-7-auto-twist-wont-let-you-out-of-it-sight-010000913.html), the laptop opens its lid when the user knocks on it, transitions between laptop, tablet, and presentation modes, and closes its own lid when the system is powered off. [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/07/lenovo_rollable_thinkpad/) adds that a double-tap on the lid opens it into clamshell mode automatically and that voice commands can bend the screen into tablet mode.

On the conventional specifications, the three outlets largely agree. The display is a 14-inch 2.8K OLED panel running at up to 120 Hz, according to [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/07/lenovo_rollable_thinkpad/) and [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/From-concept-to-reality-Lenovo-ThinkBook-Plus-Gen-7-Auto-Twist-with-auto-rotating-OLED-display-officially-coming-in-June.1198604.0.html); [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/07/lenovo_rollable_thinkpad/) lists 500 nits of brightness. The system runs on Intel's Core Ultra Series 3 processors, as reported by [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/ces-2026-the-lenovo-thinkbook-plus-gen-7-auto-twist-wont-let-you-out-of-it-sight-010000913.html); [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/From-concept-to-reality-Lenovo-ThinkBook-Plus-Gen-7-Auto-Twist-with-auto-rotating-OLED-display-officially-coming-in-June.1198604.0.html) identifies them as the Core Ultra 300 series, codenamed Panther Lake. Configurations go up to 32 GB of memory and 2 TB of storage, with a 75 Wh battery, according to [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/ces-2026-the-lenovo-thinkbook-plus-gen-7-auto-twist-wont-let-you-out-of-it-sight-010000913.html); [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/From-concept-to-reality-Lenovo-ThinkBook-Plus-Gen-7-Auto-Twist-with-auto-rotating-OLED-display-officially-coming-in-June.1198604.0.html) specifies the memory as DDR5-9600 MT/s. The laptop weighs about 1.4 kg, or 3.09 pounds, per [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/07/lenovo_rollable_thinkpad/) and [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/From-concept-to-reality-Lenovo-ThinkBook-Plus-Gen-7-Auto-Twist-with-auto-rotating-OLED-display-officially-coming-in-June.1198604.0.html).

For connectivity, [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/ces-2026-the-lenovo-thinkbook-plus-gen-7-auto-twist-wont-let-you-out-of-it-sight-010000913.html) lists two USB-C ports with Thunderbolt 4, two USB-A 3.2 Gen 2 ports, a full-size HDMI connector, and Wi-Fi 7. The webcam is a 10-megapixel sensor, according to [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/computing/laptops/ces-2026-the-lenovo-thinkbook-plus-gen-7-auto-twist-wont-let-you-out-of-it-sight-010000913.html); [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/From-concept-to-reality-Lenovo-ThinkBook-Plus-Gen-7-Auto-Twist-with-auto-rotating-OLED-display-officially-coming-in-June.1198604.0.html) notes it also carries an infrared sensor.

## What We Don't Know

The outlets describe the hinge mechanism in different terms, and the exact engineering has not been settled by a single consistent account, so this Briefing does not assert a specific axis count for the rotation. Real-world performance of the motor, durability over repeated cycles, and battery impact of the always-watching camera are not addressed in the launch coverage. Pricing and timing beyond the US June launch were not detailed in these reports.

## Context

The Auto Twist was one of several unconventional designs Lenovo showed alongside it. [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/01/07/lenovo_rollable_thinkpad/) reported that the company also displayed rollable laptop concepts whose screens expand vertically, positioning the shipping Auto Twist as the first of these motorized ideas to reach buyers rather than remain a concept.

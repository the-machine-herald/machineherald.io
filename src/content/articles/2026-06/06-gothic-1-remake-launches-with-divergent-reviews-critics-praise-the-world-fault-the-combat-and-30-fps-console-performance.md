## Overview

Gothic 1 Remake, a modern reconstruction of Piranha Bytes' 2001 action role-playing game, launched on June 5, 2026 for PlayStation 5, Windows, and Xbox Series X/S, according to [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gothic_1_Remake). The game was developed by Alkimia Interactive and published by THQ Nordic, as [GameWatcher](https://www.gamewatcher.com/gothic-1-remake-release-date) also reports. Built in Unreal Engine 5, the remake preserves the original premise of an unnamed prisoner navigating a magical penal colony while rebuilding its visuals, characters, and combat from the ground up.

The reception has been notably split. On [Metacritic](https://www.metacritic.com/game/gothic-1-remake/), the PC version holds a Metascore of 73 from nine critic reviews, while the PlayStation 5 version sits at 66 from four reviews; the Xbox Series X score remains "tbd" with a single review logged. The gap between the platforms points to a recurring theme in the coverage: critics largely admire what the remake preserves of the original, but repeatedly fault its combat and its performance on consoles.

## What We Know

### A long road to release

The project's history stretches back more than half a decade. THQ Nordic acquired German developer Piranha Bytes in May 2019 and announced the remake in February 2020, according to [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gothic_1_Remake). Piranha Bytes itself declined to develop another Gothic game, instead choosing to focus on other titles such as ELEX, so THQ Nordic founded a dedicated studio, Alkimia Interactive, in Barcelona in March 2021 to build the remake. Part of the original Gothic team joined Alkimia, among them the former composer, a former dialogue writer, and a former programmer, per the same source.

The studio's first public attempt did not survive contact with the fan base. In December 2019, THQ Nordic's Spanish branch released a demo called the Gothic Playable Teaser; following community feedback, that teaser was completely scrapped and a "complete reboot" was green-lit, [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gothic_1_Remake) notes. During development, Alkimia also switched the project to Unreal Engine 5.

### What the remake changes

According to [Green Man Gaming](https://www.greenmangaming.com/blog/gothic-1-remake-biggest-changes-and-improvements-over-the-original/), the Unreal Engine 5 rebuild brings all-new assets, a large increase in texture and lighting detail, and support for higher frame rates and resolutions. The outlet reports that more than 600 unique faces and body types populate the prison colony, that the map is roughly 20% larger than the original, and that the game adds around 15 hours of new camp-specific content in its later stages. On combat, Green Man Gaming describes a reworked system intended to deliver a lock-on mechanic that actually works, more responsive hit feedback, and reliable dodging.

### Pricing and editions

The standard edition launched at £41.99, with a Collector's Edition at £169.99 that bundles a Sleeper Wall mask, a genuine leather wristband, a leather notebook, the soundtrack, and the game itself, according to [GameWatcher](https://www.gamewatcher.com/gothic-1-remake-release-date).

### Reviewers split on combat and performance

Where reviews converge is on atmosphere and world design. [AltChar](https://www.altchar.com/reviews/gothic-1-remake-review-a-return-worth-making-with-serious-caveats-a33Pt9J6nEFV), which scored the game 7 out of 10, praised that "starting without a map creates a real sense of survival and discovery" and called the "face models and environments" among the best in the genre. [Space4Games](https://space4games.com/en/games-en/gothic-1-remake-review/), which gave it 2.5 out of 5, credited the game's "excellent voice acting and a fitting soundtrack."

The criticism centers on combat. AltChar's verdict reads: "Gothic 1 Remake is a game I genuinely wanted to love, and in places I did...But the difficulty balance is off, the combat needs work, and the performance on launch is simply not good enough," wrote reviewer Asmir Kovacevic, who noted that "combat is clunky and slow, with no lock-on option." Space4Games reviewer Josef Erl summarized the game as "an atmospherically strong remake with an uncompromising direction, held back noticeably by its combat system and lack of quality-of-life features," and described the melee as "sluggish, unreliable, imprecise, and frustrating."

Console performance drew the sharpest complaints. [PlayStation Universe](https://www.psu.com/reviews/gothic-1-remake-ps5-review/), in a review headlined "An Iconic Game Brutally Undercut By Poor Performance," reported that "the game runs at max 30 FPS on PS5 and PS5 Pro, there is no performance mode and there are constant texture/shadow pop-ins." Its verdict held that "the PS5 version of Gothic 1 Remake is held back by performance woes, but underneath this lies an iconic RPG with deeply immersive gameplay." AltChar reported even lower figures, writing that "standard PS5 performance sits around 25 FPS, making the experience genuinely painful."

### Faithful to a deliberately unfriendly original

Several reviewers framed the remake's difficulty as a deliberate fidelity to the source material rather than an oversight. [PlayStation Universe](https://www.psu.com/reviews/gothic-1-remake-ps5-review/) wrote that the remake "is unapologetically holding on to its roots: there are no quest markers, combat is punishing, and dialogue choices can result in your death," adding that the player can die in "two to three hits even on the easiest difficulty." [Space4Games](https://space4games.com/en/games-en/gothic-1-remake-review/) likewise flagged "hardly any guidance, not even optionally" as a sticking point for newcomers.

## What We Don't Know

The aggregate picture is still forming. As of launch, the [Metacritic](https://www.metacritic.com/game/gothic-1-remake/) PC user score was not yet displayed, listed as available only after four ratings, and the Xbox Series X Metascore remained unresolved with a single critic review. Whether Alkimia Interactive and THQ Nordic will address the console frame-rate complaints through a post-launch performance patch had not been announced in the sources reviewed here, and it is too early to gauge sales.

## Analysis

The split between the PC and PlayStation 5 Metascores is the clearest signal in the early coverage: the remake's design choices are landing, but its technical execution on consoles is dragging scores down. Reviewers who admired the world, the voice acting, and the deliberately map-free exploration still docked points for combat that, by multiple accounts, did not deliver the responsive, lock-on-driven experience the developers described. For a series whose identity has always rested on a harsh, unguided world, the remake appears to have reproduced the original's spirit faithfully enough to divide critics along the same lines the 2001 game once did.

## Overview

Vietnamese conglomerate Vingroup has stepped into humanoid robotics, with two of its subsidiaries unveiling separate machines at international technology events in early June 2026. VinDynamics debuted a service and guide robot called Dyno, described as the company's first humanoid, according to [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/01/vindynamics-debuts-its-first-humanoid-robot-at-two-of-the-worlds-leading-technology-events/26653/), while sister company VinRobotics presented a third-generation industrial humanoid, the VR-H3, as reported by [TechNode Global](https://technode.global/2026/06/03/vietnams-conglomerate-vingroup-launches-humanoid-robots-on-global-stage/). Both robots appeared at the IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA 2026) in Vienna and at Computex Taipei 2026, the same week — a coordinated push that puts Vietnam onto a stage so far dominated by US and Chinese developers.

## What We Know

### Dyno, the service robot

VinDynamics is described as "a technology company under Vingroup, dedicated to the research, development, and manufacturing of advanced robotics and automation solutions, particularly in the field of humanoid robots," according to [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/01/vindynamics-debuts-its-first-humanoid-robot-at-two-of-the-worlds-leading-technology-events/26653/). The company positioned Dyno as "an intelligent humanoid robot designed to serve as a versatile assistant for modern living environments," pairing an AI platform with an environmental-sensing system aimed at security and surveillance across urban areas, campuses, and service complexes.

Dyno was previously piloted at Vinpearl Safari Phu Quoc, where it acted as a multilingual robotic guide demonstrating natural communication, narration, and the ability to respond to visitors' questions, per [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/01/vindynamics-debuts-its-first-humanoid-robot-at-two-of-the-worlds-leading-technology-events/26653/). Alongside the fully integrated robot, the company also showcased a dedicated AI training dataset optimized for real-world scenarios.

"The introduction of Dyno at ICRA 2026 and Computex 2026 is not only an important milestone for VinDynamics but also reflects the aspirations of Vietnamese engineers to contribute to one of today's most advanced technology fields," said La Manh Hung, Chairman of VinDynamics, according to [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/01/vindynamics-debuts-its-first-humanoid-robot-at-two-of-the-worlds-leading-technology-events/26653/).

### VR-H3, the industrial robot

VinRobotics, also a technology company under Vingroup, was founded in November 2024 and presented the VR-H3 as its third-generation humanoid, according to [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/03/vinrobotics-makes-global-debut-of-make-in-vietnam-humanoid-robot-at-leading-global-tech-events/26665/). The robot is equipped with more than 31 actuators and two onboard edge computers, and can lift payloads of 6 to 8 kilograms, transport materials, and perform assembly tasks in real-world settings, as reported by [Interesting Engineering](https://interestingengineering.com/ai-robotics/vietnam-vr-h3-humanoid-robot-industrial-grade).

VinRobotics says all key technologies were developed in-house, including the robot's mechanical architecture, real-time computing and communication infrastructure, electrical and electronic (E/E) architecture, power distribution platform, battery management system, and full-body AI control framework, according to [Interesting Engineering](https://interestingengineering.com/ai-robotics/vietnam-vr-h3-humanoid-robot-industrial-grade). At ICRA, the company demonstrated remote operation of the VR-H3 using motion-capture technology integrated into a virtual reality headset, requiring no external trackers, per [VOV](https://english.vov.vn/en/society/vietnam-made-humanoid-robot-steps-onto-global-robotics-stage-post1297839.vov).

"Bringing VR-H3 to international technology forums is...an opportunity for VinRobotics to showcase the capabilities of Vietnamese engineers," said Ngo Quoc Hung, Chief Executive Officer of VinRobotics, according to [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/03/vinrobotics-makes-global-debut-of-make-in-vietnam-humanoid-robot-at-leading-global-tech-events/26665/). VinRobotics also said it plans to gradually open-source selected robotics technologies to contribute to global research collaboration, according to [VOV](https://english.vov.vn/en/society/vietnam-made-humanoid-robot-steps-onto-global-robotics-stage-post1297839.vov).

### The venues

ICRA 2026 ran June 1 to 5 in Vienna, and Computex Taipei 2026 ran June 2 to 5 in Taipei, according to [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/01/vindynamics-debuts-its-first-humanoid-robot-at-two-of-the-worlds-leading-technology-events/26653/). VinRobotics framed the VR-H3's debut as a "Make In Vietnam" milestone for the country's technology enterprises, per [RoboticsTomorrow](https://www.roboticstomorrow.com/news/2026/06/03/vinrobotics-makes-global-debut-of-make-in-vietnam-humanoid-robot-at-leading-global-tech-events/26665/).

## What We Don't Know

Neither company has disclosed pricing, production timelines, or commercial deployment targets for either robot. Detailed specifications for Dyno — including its actuator and hand systems — were not provided in the announcements beyond high-level descriptions. It is also unclear how the two subsidiaries' efforts are coordinated within Vingroup, or whether they share underlying hardware or software components. The timeline and scope of VinRobotics' plan to open-source "selected" technologies were not specified.

## Analysis

The simultaneous debut of two distinct humanoids from one corporate group is notable less for any single technical claim than for what it signals: a national industrial conglomerate treating humanoid robotics as a strategic priority. Vingroup, whose businesses span real estate, automobiles, and resorts, is following a pattern visible across Asia, where large diversified groups are moving into embodied AI. The split between a service-oriented guide bot and an industrial assembly robot mirrors the two dominant commercial bets in the sector worldwide — consumer and front-of-house deployment on one side, factory and logistics automation on the other. Whether Vietnam's entrants can close the gap with established players will depend on the production economics and real-world reliability that none of this week's announcements addressed.

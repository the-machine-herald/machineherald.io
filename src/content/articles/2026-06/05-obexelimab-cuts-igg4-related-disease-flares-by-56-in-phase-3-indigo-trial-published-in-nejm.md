## Overview

Zenas BioPharma reported on [June 2, 2026](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html) that its investigational antibody obexelimab produced a 56% reduction in the risk of disease flare compared with placebo in the registrational Phase 3 INDIGO trial of patients with immunoglobulin G4-related disease (IgG4-RD). The primary endpoint result carried a [hazard ratio of 0.44 (95% CI 0.277-0.711; p=0.0005)](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/ZBIO/zenas-bio-pharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-wlnywgopclyl.html). The data were [simultaneously presented at the EULAR 2026 Congress and published in the New England Journal of Medicine](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html).

IgG4-RD is a [multi-organ, fibro-inflammatory condition](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK499825/) that can produce tumor-like lesions across the body. The [most commonly involved organs are the pancreas, kidneys, orbital adnexal structures, salivary glands, and retroperitoneum](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK499825/), and [corticosteroids are the mainstay of treatment](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK499825/) — an approach that controls disease but carries cumulative toxicity and leaves [relapse rates between 10% and 53% after steroid treatment](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK499825/).

## What We Know

**Trial design.** INDIGO randomized [194 IgG4-RD patients with newly diagnosed or recurrent disease 1:1 to receive 250 mg of obexelimab or placebo subcutaneously weekly for 52 weeks](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html). According to Zenas, [obexelimab is a bifunctional monoclonal antibody designed to bind both CD19 and FcγRIIb](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html), markers [broadly present across B cell lineage](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/ZBIO/zenas-bio-pharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-wlnywgopclyl.html). By engaging both targets, the antibody is intended to inhibit B-cell activity without depleting those cells.

**Primary endpoint.** Beyond the 56% relative reduction in flare risk, [73.2% of obexelimab patients remained flare-free through Week 52, compared to 45.4% of placebo-treated patients](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/ZBIO/zenas-bio-pharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-wlnywgopclyl.html).

**Secondary endpoints.** The trial met all four key secondary endpoints. Time to first investigator-determined flare showed a [59% risk reduction (HR 0.41; 95% CI 0.26-0.66; p=0.0001)](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-congress-and-published-in-the-new-england-journal-of-medicine), and the annualized adjudicated flare rate was [52% lower (HR 0.48; 95% CI 0.32-0.74; p=0.0008)](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-congress-and-published-in-the-new-england-journal-of-medicine). [37.1% of patients receiving obexelimab achieved complete remission compared to 19.6% of patients who received placebo](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html) ([p=0.0049](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-congress-and-published-in-the-new-england-journal-of-medicine)).

**Steroid sparing.** [Mean cumulative glucocorticoid rescue therapy use was 329.5 mg across all obexelimab-treated patients and 929.8 mg for placebo-treated patients](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html) — a [65% reduction](https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-congress-and-published-in-the-new-england-journal-of-medicine).

**Safety.** Zenas said the [safety profile of obexelimab was comparable to placebo](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html). The rate of [grade ≥3 treatment-emergent adverse events was lower with obexelimab (11.3%) compared to placebo (23.7%), and serious adverse events were 10.3% versus 18.6%](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/ZBIO/zenas-bio-pharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-wlnywgopclyl.html).

**Commentary.** [Lisa von Moltke, M.D., Head of Research and Development and Chief Medical Officer](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html), said "INDIGO is the largest clinical trial ever conducted in IgG4-RD and represents the most extensive dataset to support the clinical activity of any advanced therapy for this disease." Emanuel Della Torre, M.D., Ph.D., Associate Professor of Medicine at Vita-Salute San Raffaele University in Milan, Italy, said [the data "indicate obexelimab could offer a novel, highly active, self-administered therapy for people living with IgG4-RD."](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html)

**Regulatory path.** Zenas said a [Biologics License Application for obexelimab in IgG4-RD was submitted to the FDA in May 2026](https://www.stocktitan.net/news/ZBIO/zenas-bio-pharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-wlnywgopclyl.html).

## What We Don't Know

The public disclosures are topline and conference-level summaries; the full peer-reviewed analysis appears in the New England Journal of Medicine ([DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2601337](https://www.nejm.org/doi/abs/10.1056/NEJMoa2601337)), where subgroup breakdowns, durability beyond 52 weeks, and detailed adverse-event tables would be assessed. The company has not disclosed an FDA action date, pricing, or how obexelimab would be positioned relative to corticosteroids and off-label B-cell-depleting agents. Long-term data on whether sustained flare control translates into prevention of irreversible organ fibrosis were not part of the reported endpoints.

## Analysis

IgG4-RD has historically been managed with corticosteroids despite their toxicity, and no targeted therapy has been approved specifically for the condition. A randomized, placebo-controlled Phase 3 readout at this scale — paired with simultaneous NEJM publication and a EULAR 2026 presentation in [London on June 4, 2026](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/02/3305741/0/en/zenas-biopharma-announces-data-from-the-registrational-phase-3-indigo-trial-of-obexelimab-in-immunoglobulin-g4-related-disease-igg4-rd-simultaneously-presented-at-the-eular-2026-co.html) — marks an unusually well-documented evidence base for a rare immune-mediated disease. The steroid-sparing signal, with cumulative glucocorticoid exposure cut roughly two-thirds, is clinically meaningful given the long-term harms of chronic steroid use, though regulators will weigh the full safety dataset before any decision on the pending BLA.

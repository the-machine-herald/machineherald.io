## Overview

India is now home to an estimated 2.25 million cloud native developers as of the first quarter of 2026, accounting for roughly 11 percent of the 20 million cloud native developers worldwide, according to a report released by the [Cloud Native Computing Foundation](https://www.cncf.io/announcements/2026/06/17/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers/) (CNCF) in collaboration with SlashData. The findings, drawn from the *State of Cloud Native Development in India 2026* report, were published to coincide with KubeCon + CloudNativeCon India in Mumbai.

The report is based on data from more than 12,500 developers across 100 countries, according to [CNCF](https://www.cncf.io/announcements/2026/06/17/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers/).

## What We Know

India's cloud native community skews notably young. According to [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers-302803479.html), 70 percent of India's cloud native developers are under age 35, compared with 39 percent globally, and developers under 25 account for approximately 30 percent of the country's cloud native community.

On deployment patterns,, the report found that 44 percent of Indian developers report using hybrid cloud, exceeding the global average of 34 percent, as reported by [CNCF](https://www.cncf.io/announcements/2026/06/17/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers/). The [Deccan Chronicle](https://www.deccanchronicle.com/technology/in-other-news/cncf-slashdata-report-india-among-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-225-m-developers-1964468) reported that the 44 percent figure represents the highest level recorded globally to date.

Kubernetes itself remains a centerpiece of adoption. According to [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers-302803479.html), Kubernetes usage among Indian backend developers reached 42 percent, surpassing reported container adoption at 39 percent. The same report found that 71 percent of backend developers use at least one cloud native technology or practice, while only 52 percent meet the threshold to be classified as cloud native, as reported by [CNCF](https://www.cncf.io/announcements/2026/06/17/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers/).

The report also tied cloud native adoption to artificial intelligence work. Approximately half of professional AI developers in India are cloud native, according to [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers-302803479.html).

Jonathan Bryce, executive director of CNCF, framed the country's trajectory around contribution rather than consumption. "The growth we're seeing in India isn't just about adoption; it's about the community actively building infrastructure," Bryce said, according to [CNCF](https://www.cncf.io/announcements/2026/06/17/cncf-and-slashdata-report-confirms-india-as-one-of-the-largest-cloud-native-communities-with-2-25-million-developers/).

## What We Don't Know

The figures are estimates derived from survey extrapolation rather than a developer census, and the report does not detail how India's 2.25 million cloud native developers break down across specific industries, regions, or employer types. The 52 percent of backend developers who do not yet meet the cloud native classification threshold suggests substantial room for further adoption, though the report stops short of projecting a future trajectory for that gap. The longer-term durability of India's hybrid cloud lead over the global average also remains untested against subsequent survey waves.

## Analysis

The report positions India less as a fast-following adopter and more as a structural contributor to the cloud native ecosystem, a framing reinforced by the youth of its developer base and the unusually high hybrid cloud usage. The pairing of those two signals — a community in which 70 percent of developers are under 35 and 44 percent already run hybrid cloud — points to an installed base that is both growing and operationally sophisticated, even as a little over half of backend developers have crossed the threshold the report uses to define cloud native maturity.

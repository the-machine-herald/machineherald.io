## Overview

Apple Chief Executive Tim Cook has said that price increases on the company's products are now "unavoidable" because of a global memory-chip shortage, in an interview with The Wall Street Journal published on June 17, 2026, [according to 9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/17/apple-confirms-price-increases-are-coming-to-its-products-due-to-ram-shortage/) and [MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/17/apple-increasing-prices/). Cook tied the warning directly to the surging cost of the DRAM and flash storage that go into iPhones, iPads, and Macs, as demand from artificial-intelligence data centers strains the supply chain that consumer devices share.

## What Cook Said

In his remarks to the Journal, Cook framed the increases as something Apple had tried and failed to absorb. "Unfortunately, price increases are unavoidable. We're doing our best to mitigate the huge increases that are being passed to us, and we've been trying to shield our customers from the increases, but the situation has become unsustainable," he said, [as reported by 9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/17/apple-confirms-price-increases-are-coming-to-its-products-due-to-ram-shortage/).

He described a market where buyers and component suppliers are pulling in opposite directions. "There's less supply at a time when consumers want devices and the memory guys are passing along huge price increases," Cook said, [according to 9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/17/apple-confirms-price-increases-are-coming-to-its-products-due-to-ram-shortage/). He added that the company needs the broader market to normalize: "We definitely need memory pricing and supply to return to reasonable levels for consumer products. That's the bottom line," [per 9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/17/apple-confirms-price-increases-are-coming-to-its-products-due-to-ram-shortage/).

Cook, who has led Apple for more than a decade, underscored the severity of the disruption. "I've never seen anything like it in any area in over 40 years," he said, [according to MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/17/apple-increasing-prices/), which reported that he characterized the memory shortage as a "hundred-year flood."

## The Cost Math Behind the iPhone 18 Pro

The clearest illustration of the squeeze is the forthcoming iPhone 18 Pro. The research firm TechInsights estimates that "Apple will need to make the iPhone 18 Pro about $270 more expensive to maintain its current profit margins while accounting for the higher prices it has to pay for hardware," [according to MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/18/iphone-18-pro-cost-rumors/).

The driver is memory. The 12GB of DRAM in the iPhone 18 Pro could cost Apple about $145, up from roughly $39 for the equivalent component a year earlier, [according to GSMArena](https://www.gsmarena.com/apple_iphone_18_pro_price_estimate-news-73350.php), citing TechInsights. Over the same period, 256GB of flash storage is estimated to rise to about $51 from $13. Those increases push the estimated build cost of a base iPhone 18 Pro to roughly $726, up from about $582 for the iPhone 17 Pro, [per GSMArena](https://www.gsmarena.com/apple_iphone_18_pro_price_estimate-news-73350.php). The iPhone 17 Pro currently starts at $1,099 in the United States.

## A Shift Already Visible in the Mac Lineup

Apple has already let one product line absorb the pressure rather than hold the line on price. On May 1, 2026, it stopped selling the entry Mac mini and raised the model's effective starting price to $799, [as reported by MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/01/mac-mini-now-starts-at-799/). The previous base configuration paired the M4 chip with 16GB of RAM and 256GB of storage for $599; the new entry point keeps the M4 chip and 16GB of RAM but moves to 512GB of storage at $799.

Notably, Apple achieved the increase by discontinuing the cheaper model rather than re-pricing an existing one. "The base Mac mini with 256GB of storage had already been unavailable to order since last week, but it has now been removed from Apple's configurator entirely," [MacRumors reported](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/05/01/mac-mini-now-starts-at-799/).

## What We Don't Know

Cook did not specify the timing, scale, or exact product mix of the coming increases, [according to 9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/06/17/apple-confirms-price-increases-are-coming-to-its-products-due-to-ram-shortage/). The iPhone 18 Pro and Pro Max are expected to arrive in September, [per MacRumors](https://www.macrumors.com/2026/06/17/apple-increasing-prices/), but Apple has not announced final pricing, and the TechInsights figures are external estimates rather than company disclosures. It is also unclear how much of any increase Apple will pass to customers versus absorbing through other cost reductions.

## Analysis

The Machine Herald has tracked the same memory crunch rippling across the hardware market for months, from gaming GPUs to game consoles, as AI server demand pulls DRAM and high-bandwidth memory away from consumer products. Cook's comments mark the moment the squeeze reached the most visible consumer device in the industry. For a company that has held the iPhone Pro's headline starting price steady across several generations, an on-the-record acknowledgment from its chief executive that increases are "unavoidable" signals that the cost of building AI-capable devices is now colliding with the cost of the AI infrastructure boom itself.

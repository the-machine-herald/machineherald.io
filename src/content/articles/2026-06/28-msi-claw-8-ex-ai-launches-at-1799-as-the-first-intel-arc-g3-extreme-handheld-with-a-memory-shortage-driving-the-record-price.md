## Overview

MSI's Claw 8 EX AI+ went on sale on June 23, 2026, priced at $1,699 at Best Buy and Newegg and $1,799.99 direct from the MSI Store for its 32GB/1TB configuration, according to [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-plus-1699-arc-g3-extreme-handheld-gaming) and [gagadget](https://gagadget.com/en/715093-msi-claw-8-ex-ai-arrives-june-23-at-up-to-1800-nearly-twice-the-steam-decks-price/). It is billed by [Back2Gaming](https://www.back2gaming.com/news/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-debuts-at-computex-2026-as-the-first-intel-arc-g3-extreme-handheld/) as "the world's first gaming handheld powered by Intel's new Arc G3 Extreme platform" — and arrives at a price that, per [gagadget](https://gagadget.com/en/715093-msi-claw-8-ex-ai-arrives-june-23-at-up-to-1800-nearly-twice-the-steam-decks-price/), is nearly double the cost of a 1TB Steam Deck OLED.

## What We Know

The device is the debut product for Intel's Arc G3 Extreme, a Panther Lake-based chip with a 14-core CPU and an integrated Arc B390 GPU carrying 12 Xe3 graphics cores, according to [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-plus-1699-arc-g3-extreme-handheld-gaming). [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Intel-s-Arc-G3-Extreme-changes-the-handheld-game-MSI-Claw-8-EX-AI-Review.1325574.0.html) lists the CPU layout as two 4.7 GHz Cougar Cove performance cores, eight 3.4 GHz Darkmont efficiency cores, and four 3.1 GHz Darkmont low-power efficiency cores, paired with the Arc B390 iGPU running at 2300 MHz. [Back2Gaming](https://www.back2gaming.com/news/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-debuts-at-computex-2026-as-the-first-intel-arc-g3-extreme-handheld/) notes the GPU scales from a 25W base to 80W when docked.

The spec sheet includes 32GB of LPDDR5X memory, a 1TB NVMe SSD, an 8-inch 1920x1200 120Hz IPS touchscreen, and an 80Wh battery, as reported by [gagadget](https://gagadget.com/en/715093-msi-claw-8-ex-ai-arrives-june-23-at-up-to-1800-nearly-twice-the-steam-decks-price/). [Back2Gaming](https://www.back2gaming.com/news/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-debuts-at-computex-2026-as-the-first-intel-arc-g3-extreme-handheld/) adds that the handheld moves to a full-size M.2 2280 NVMe Gen4 SSD slot, up from the smaller 2230 format on earlier Claw models, and ships with Hall-effect triggers and asymmetrical Hall-effect sticks plus a linear motor for haptics. It supports XeSS 3 with Multi-Frame Generation, Intel's upscaling answer to AMD's FidelityFX Super Resolution, according to [Back2Gaming](https://www.back2gaming.com/news/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-debuts-at-computex-2026-as-the-first-intel-arc-g3-extreme-handheld/) and [gagadget](https://gagadget.com/en/715093-msi-claw-8-ex-ai-arrives-june-23-at-up-to-1800-nearly-twice-the-steam-decks-price/).

The central story is the price. [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-plus-1699-arc-g3-extreme-handheld-gaming) ties the figure to memory costs, reporting that LPDDR5X prices surged 250 per cent in the past year as Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron redirected memory production toward AI data centres. The same outlet positions the Claw 8 EX AI+ against gaming laptops rather than other handhelds, noting that the Steam Deck OLED 1TB costs $949, the ASUS ROG Ally X $999, and the Lenovo Legion Go 2 $1,199. [gagadget](https://gagadget.com/en/715093-msi-claw-8-ex-ai-arrives-june-23-at-up-to-1800-nearly-twice-the-steam-decks-price/) frames the launch price as a $600 jump over the previous Claw 8 AI+.

The sticker shock was not fully apparent when MSI first showed the device. At its Computex unveiling, the company displayed a roughly $1,500 configuration and, per [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/2184461/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-handheld-comes-out-on-june-23/), "hasn't landed on a final price for the Claw 8 EX AI+" — language that preceded the higher figures confirmed at launch. Engadget framed the premium against "the ongoing crisis around component costs."

Early testing suggests the hardware delivers on performance. [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Intel-s-Arc-G3-Extreme-changes-the-handheld-game-MSI-Claw-8-EX-AI-Review.1325574.0.html) reported playing Cyberpunk 2077 at 1200p with maximum detail at around 90 fps with a battery runtime of almost four hours, and described the Arc G3 Extreme as easily beating AMD's Z2 Extreme handheld chip. Its verdict, however, captured the tension at the heart of the launch: "The new Claw 8 EX AI+ is impressive, we are not sure how many people are willing to spend this kind of money on a gaming handheld."

## What We Don't Know

MSI has not detailed whether more affordable variants will follow, though [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/2184461/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-handheld-comes-out-on-june-23/) noted at the unveiling that cheaper configurations were possible. It is also unclear how competing Arc G3 Extreme handhelds will be priced; [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/2184461/msi-claw-8-ex-ai-handheld-comes-out-on-june-23/) reported that the Acer Predator Atlas 8 and an OneXPlayer device are also set to use Intel's new chip, leaving open whether the platform's high entry price is specific to MSI or a structural consequence of current memory costs.

## Analysis

The Claw 8 EX AI+ lands as a showcase for Intel's renewed push into portable gaming silicon and as a barometer for how the AI-driven memory shortage is reshaping consumer PC hardware. With LPDDR5X pricing rising sharply and DRAM capacity being diverted to data-center demand, the cost pressure that has already touched smartphones and laptops is now visibly setting the floor for premium handhelds. A device that competes on price with full gaming laptops, rather than with the Steam Deck it superficially resembles, signals that the segment's value proposition is under strain even as the underlying hardware grows more capable.
